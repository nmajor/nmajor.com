# Raw capture — Lokken v. UnitedHealth complaint & what nH Predict is (fetched 2026-07-07)

## Primary: Complaint PDF (Georgetown Health Care Litigation Tracker copy)
https://litigationtracker.law.georgetown.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Estate-of-Gene-B.-Lokken-et-al_20231114_COMPLAINT.pdf
- Full caption: *The Estate of Gene B. Lokken, et al. v. UnitedHealth Group, Inc., naviHealth, Inc., and UnitedHealthcare, Inc.*, Case No. 0:23-cv-03514 (D. Minn.), filed **November 14, 2023**, 46 pages.
- nH Predict is pleaded as a **"nH Predict AI Model"** — a proprietary **predictive algorithm**. The complaint does NOT describe it as a large language model or generative AI; it is a case-matching / length-of-stay **predictive statistical model**.
- Allegation (mark as ALLEGATION, not fact): nH Predict has a "~90% error rate," which plaintiffs calculate from the share of denials reversed on appeal.
- Allegation: reliance on nH Predict "in lieu of physicians" to make coverage determinations, overriding treating physicians' recommendations for continued care.
- Named plaintiff Gene B. Lokken: 91-year-old, fractured leg/ankle May 2022, transferred to post-acute rehab; UHC covered only July 1–20, 2022 (19 days) before cutting off.

## How nH Predict actually works (per complaint + STAT reporting cited in it)
- Built on a **database of ~6 million patients**. It matches a specific patient (by diagnosis, age, living situation, physical function) to "similar patients," then generates an estimated length of stay / discharge date and predicted care needs.
- => Technology type: **a predictive / actuarial case-matching model (traditional ML-style prediction), NOT an LLM and NOT generative AI.** It is a DIFFERENT, older system from UnitedHealth's current 2026 generative-AI push.

## Error-rate figures — the exact framing (ALLEGATIONS, from plaintiffs)
Per multiple summaries of the complaint (Tressler LLP, ArentFox Schiff, Healthcare Finance News):
- Plaintiffs allege UHC "knew of nH Predict's inaccuracies because **over 90% of claim denials were reversed on appeal, and over 80% of preauthorization denials were reversed**." (The "90% error rate" is derived from the reversal-on-appeal rate.)
- Plaintiffs allege the companies denied claims "knowing that only a tiny percentage — **0.2%** — would file appeals."
- IMPORTANT: These are complaint allegations, not adjudicated findings. No court has found the 90%/80% figures true.
