# Akpinar & Berger, "Valuable Virality" (JMR, 2017) — sharing vs. carrying the message

Sources:
- Baylor Keller Center research summary (with author Q&A): https://kellercenter.hankamer.baylor.edu/news/story/2018/valuable-virality
- Synthesis w/ study stimuli described: https://lilysmade.wordpress.com/2023/07/19/social-media-series-what-drives-virality
- Mannheim thesis citing it: https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/media/Lehrstuehle/bwl/Stahl/mastersthesis/MKT_720_Research_Seminar_Thesis_3.pdf

Citation: Akpinar, E., & Berger, J. (2017). "Valuable Virality." Journal of Marketing
Research, 54(2), 318-330.

## The problem it addresses
Lots of content goes viral WITHOUT doing anything for the creator/brand — the
emotional shell travels but the message doesn't ride along. This paper is about
making virality VALUABLE: shares that also move brand evaluation, not just shares.

## Core findings (verbatim / near-verbatim)
- Field study of 240 viral ads: **ad type and "brand integralness" were NEGATIVELY
  related** — advertisers tend to trade off emotion against putting the brand at the
  center of the story. Most emotional viral ads keep the brand peripheral, on the
  fear that a prominent brand kills shares.
- **That fear is wrong.** Across experiments comparing (1) emotional ads where the
  brand is INTEGRAL to the narrative, (2) emotional ads where the brand is absent/
  peripheral, and (3) informative ads: **making the brand integral did NOT reduce
  sharing** — the brand-integral emotional ads got a similar number of shares AND
  produced better brand evaluation / purchase likelihood.
- Only <20% of the viral ads examined made the brand an integral part — a large
  missed opportunity.
- Key definition: **"brand integralness"** = whether the shareable emotional hook is
  structurally tied to the message, so you cannot retell/reshare the story without
  also carrying the point. When the emotion and the message are ONE thing, the share
  carries both.

## Relevance to the breakout question — the anti-hype guardrail
This is the study that separates a USEFUL breakout from a vanity breakout. A post can
go to 170k impressions on pure emotional shock while teaching the audience nothing
about the author's actual expertise or worldview — 170k people feel a jolt, none of
them remember who you are or what you stand for. "Valuable virality" says: engineer
the breakout so the shareable hook is INTEGRAL to nmajor.com's real message
(governance, accountability, plain-English risk, "the AI is the last step").

Post A does this reasonably well: the story cannot be retold without carrying Nick's
actual thesis (unmanaged AI + missing governance = the real risk). The emotion (a
first-ever filing, a data leak) and the message (govern your AI access) are the same
object — so the reshare propagates the point, not just the shock. The design
principle for future breakout attempts: never bolt an anti-hype lesson onto an
unrelated shocking hook; make the true, surprising incident BE the lesson, so every
reshare carries the brand's worldview for free.
