# EU AI Act — Article 14 human oversight + Annex III (credit/insurance) + Digital Omnibus deferral

## Primary: Article 14 (Human oversight) — official EU AI Act Service Desk
URL: https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-14
Captured: 2026-07-07

Art. 14(1) (verbatim):
> "High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate
> human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during
> the period in which they are in use."

Art. 14(4) — the human overseer must be enabled to (verbatim fragments):
> (a) "properly understand the relevant capacities and limitations of the high-risk AI system and
> be able to duly monitor its operation, including in view of detecting and addressing anomalies,
> dysfunctions and unexpected performance";
> (b) remain aware of automation bias ("automation complacency");
> (c) "correctly interpret the high-risk AI system's output...";
> (d) "decide, in any particular situation, not to use the high-risk AI system or to otherwise
> disregard, override or reverse the output...";
> (e) "intervene in the operation of the high-risk AI system or interrupt the system through a
> 'stop' button or a similar procedure that allows the system to come to a halt in a safe state."

## Which decisions this covers — Annex III (high-risk categories)
URL: https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/annex/3/
- Annex III point 5(b): AI "intended to be used to evaluate the creditworthiness of natural
  persons or establish their credit score" (excludes fraud-detection). => CREDIT SCORING is high-risk.
- Annex III point 5(c): AI "intended to be used for risk assessment and pricing in relation to
  natural persons in the case of life and health insurance." => LIFE/HEALTH INSURANCE
  pricing/risk-assessment is high-risk.
- Annex III point 4: AI for recruitment/employment decisions (hiring, promotion, task allocation,
  monitoring) => EMPLOYMENT is high-risk.
=> For these, Art. 14 human-oversight obligations attach to the deployer/provider.

## CRITICAL TIMING NUANCE — Digital Omnibus deferral (REPORTED, provisional)
Original date: high-risk Annex III obligations were to apply from **2 August 2026** (24 months
after entry into force).
- On **7 May 2026** the Council and European Parliament reached a **political/provisional
  agreement** on the "Digital Omnibus," the first amendment package to the AI Act.
- It **defers high-risk obligations for standalone Annex III systems** (incl. credit scoring,
  insurance pricing, hiring) **from 2 August 2026 to 2 December 2027**; embedded high-risk in
  Annex I regulated products deferred to 2 August 2028.
- NOT yet law: takes legal effect only on formal adoption + publication in the Official Journal
  (expected before/around 2 Aug 2026). Article 50 transparency duties and Article 4 AI-literacy
  duty are UNCHANGED.

## Assessment
Art. 14 human-oversight text and the fact that credit-scoring / life-health insurance pricing /
hiring are Annex III "high-risk" are CONFIRMED (primary). The requirement to keep a human able to
override is real and hard-wired into the law. BUT the **application date for these categories is
now the moving part**: pre-Omnibus it was 2 Aug 2026; post-Omnibus (provisional, 7 May 2026) it
is **2 December 2027**. So honest framing: "The EU has legislated a human-override requirement for
AI credit-scoring and insurance-pricing decisions; its application date was pushed from Aug 2026
to Dec 2027 by the 2026 Digital Omnibus (pending formal adoption)." Don't state it as already
in force for insurers/lenders.
