# Kirsten Powers (Substack) — Hook The Reader With A Strong Lede

Source: https://kirstenpowers.substack.com/p/hook-the-reader-with-a-strong-lede
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Core Principles
A powerful opening should be clear, succinct, and intriguing—making readers curious to learn more. The lede must establish what the piece is about within the first sentence or two.

## Essential Techniques
- Be Direct, Not Meandering: Avoid slow wind-ups or journal-entry openings. Skip superfluous details and jump to your point immediately. Don't make readers scan to find your topic.
- Speak with Authority: Own your expertise without equivocation or apologies. "There is no way to be an interesting writer, thinker, or anything really and please everyone."
- Create Provocation: Effective ledes appeal to those who agree while catching attention from those who don't, generating engagement across perspectives.

## Strong Opening Examples (verbatim)
- Emma Gannon: "This Substack you're reading now, The Hyphen, accidentally turned into a 6-figure business last year."
- Powers' viral essay: "I don't remember exactly when it happened for me, but the thought arose with surprising clarity: something is deeply wrong..."
- Academic lede (Hedgehog Review): Opens with historical context about leisure in Samoan societies, immediately revealing the essay's focus.

## Common Mistakes
- Opening with dense paragraphs before stating your purpose
- Lengthy preambles explaining how you conceived the topic
- Hedging language or excessive qualifications
- Burying your premise beneath background information

## ANALYST NOTE
Highly brand-aligned: directness, no hedging, get to the point. The Emma Gannon opener is a good model of specific + concrete + slightly surprising without hype.
