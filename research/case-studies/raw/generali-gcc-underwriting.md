Headline metric:
- "Pre-bind underwriting activities became 80% faster, enabling quicker responses to customers
  and brokers, and accelerating product launches." (vendor-reported)

Geographic scale:
- 25 countries (Generali GC&C = Global Corporate & Commercial line).

Technology:
- Platform: AGORA, a cloud-native SaaS built on Microsoft tech (Dynamics 365, Power Platform,
  SharePoint Online).
- Gen AI-powered assistant provides on-demand info about the platform, automates everyday tasks,
  handles service requests.

Additional quantified benefits (all vendor-reported):
- 60% reduction in software licensing costs.
- 75% reduction in support and maintenance staffing costs.
- 75% reduction in service desk tickets within first three months post-launch.
- 1.9 TB of document content migrated to SharePoint Online.

Productivity:
- "Streamlined workflows and automation freed up underwriters to handle more submissions,
  significantly increasing productivity" — no percentage given.

Timeline / approach:
- Launched as MVP with core pre-bind functionality, then quarterly updates. No explicit project dates.

Caveat: this is a Capgemini-authored client story. The 80% is "pre-bind underwriting activities,"
a process-step time measure, not an end-to-end underwriting or loss-ratio outcome. The Gen AI piece
is one component of a broader SaaS/Microsoft migration; the licensing/support savings are platform
consolidation, not AI per se.
