# Benchmark Email — Clickbait Email Subject Lines and Subscriber Trust

Source: https://www.benchmarkemail.com/blog/clickbait-email-subject-lines/
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## What Damages Trust
- Misleading content: "When subscribers feel misled, they are more likely to unsubscribe or disengage with the brand altogether."
- Unmet expectations: "Clickbait can lead to disappointment when subscribers realize the email content does not live up to their expectations. This practice can erode trust, lead to decreased engagement."
- Brand reputation: "Recipients may perceive the brand as manipulative or lacking integrity. A brand associated with clickbait and sensationalism may struggle to be taken seriously."

## Concrete Consequences
- Increased likelihood of recipients marking emails as spam
- Harm to sender reputation and deliverability rates
- Higher spam complaint rates affecting future campaign success

## Clickbait Examples (AVOID)
- "You won't believe what's inside this email!"
- "This shocking news will change your life!"
- "You've won a prize – open to claim it now!"

## Honest Subject Line Alternatives
- "Discover 5 Expert Tips to Boost Your Productivity"
- "Limited-Time Sale: Get 20% Off Your Favorite Products"
- "Unlock Exclusive Insights from Industry Leaders"

Core recommendation: "Deliver on the promises made in subject lines and provide valuable content."

## ANALYST NOTE
Strong evidence for the anti-clickbait brand thesis. Note even the "honest alternatives" here lean on marketing words ("Unlock Exclusive Insights") that this brand would still soften.
