# Journalism Leads (types) + Morning Brew / The Hustle lead approaches

## SOURCE 1: Journalism lead types (search synthesis + Fiveable/Pressbooks/MasterClass)
Sources:
- https://pressbooks.pub/introjournalism/chapter/chapter-8-leads/ (403 on fetch; content via search)
- https://fiveable.me/introduction-journalism/unit-7/lead-writing-types-leads/study-guide/Ghyqb1kyJ86nEp6H
- https://www.masterclass.com/articles/how-to-write-a-lede-in-journalism
Accessed: 2026-06-19

- Lead/lede = first paragraph; classic 5 Ws + H (who, what, when, where, why, how).
- Summary Lead (straight/direct): delivers essential points immediately. Best for breaking news, government actions, "any story where the facts themselves are the hook."
- Anecdotal Lead: opens with a short, specific story about a real person before broadening to the larger topic. Puts a human face on the news. Common in features/profiles.
  Example: "'I lost everything,' said John Smith, standing in front of the charred remains of his apartment. Smith is one of 20 residents left homeless by a massive fire that tore through the downtown complex last night."
- Best practices:
  - "Write your lead, then count the words. If you're over 35, look for phrases you can trim."
  - "Read it out loud... If you stumble while reading, your reader will stumble too."
  - "Your lead should accurately reflect what the rest of the article covers. If the lead promises one angle but the story delivers another, something needs to change." (anti-bait at body level)

## SOURCE 2: Morning Brew & The Hustle (search synthesis)
Sources: dupple.com, monday.com, failory.com (best newsletter examples roundups)
Accessed: 2026-06-19
- Morning Brew: a "witty editor's note" — a single short, voicey paragraph from the day's editor before the news starts. Humanizes the product; cited as the single biggest reason people open instead of skim.
- The Hustle: a "Big Idea" lead — every issue opens with one main business story told as a NARRATIVE, not a roundup. The narrative lead pulls readers in before they bounce to social.
- The Hustle uses informal language, puns, witty humor.

## ANALYST NOTE
The "lead must match the article" rule = anti-clickbait applied to the lede. Morning Brew/Hustle voice (puns, "dude/damn") is NOT brand-aligned (too casual/hype-adjacent), BUT the structural insight transfers: open with ONE narrative/idea in a real human voice, not a table-of-contents. The 35-word-max lead test is a concrete, brand-friendly editing rule.
