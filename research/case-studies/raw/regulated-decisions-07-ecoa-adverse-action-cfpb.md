# ECOA / Reg B adverse-action "specific reasons" — applies to AI credit denials (CFPB)

Captured: 2026-07-07

## Primary: CFPB Circular 2023-03 (Sept 19, 2023)
URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/compliance/circulars/circular-2023-03-adverse-action-notification-requirements-and-the-proper-use-of-the-cfpbs-sample-forms-provided-in-regulation-b/
Federal Register version (published Apr 17, 2024): https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/04/17/2024-08003/

What it establishes:
- Under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Regulation B, a creditor that takes adverse
  action (e.g., denies credit) must give the applicant a statement of the **specific and accurate
  principal reason(s)** for the decision.
- CFPB: creditors "may not rely on the checklist of reasons provided in sample forms" if those
  reasons "do not specifically and accurately indicate the principal reason(s)," nor use "overly
  broad or vague reasons that obscure the specific and accurate reasons."
- Applies fully when the decision is made by **AI / complex algorithms**, including models using
  data from outside the applicant's credit file/application. Consumers "may not anticipate that
  certain data gathered outside of their application or credit file ... may be a principal reason."

## Companion: CFPB Circular 2022-03 (May 26, 2022) — the "no black-box exemption" rule
URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/compliance/circulars/circular-2022-03-adverse-action-notification-requirements-in-connection-with-credit-decisions-based-on-complex-algorithms/
- "There is no exception [from ECOA/Reg B] for violating the law because a creditor is using
  technology that is too complicated, too opaque, or too new." A creditor's lack of understanding
  of its own model is not a defense.
Corroboration:
- CFPB newsroom (Sept 19, 2023): https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/newsroom/cfpb-issues-guidance-on-credit-denials-by-lenders-using-artificial-intelligence/
- Skadden analysis: https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2024/01/cfpb-applies-adverse-action-notification-requirement

## Assessment (CONFIRMED, in force)
Strong, in-force US federal fact. Precise framing: ECOA/Reg B doesn't literally require a human to
make the decision — it requires that whoever/whatever makes it must produce **specific, accurate
reasons a person can see**, and the CFPB has said an AI/black-box model gets NO pass on that. So
it's the "explainability/accountability is legally mandatory for AI credit denials" fact rather
than a pure "human in the loop." Caveat: these are CFPB *circulars/guidance* (statements of the
Bureau's enforcement view of existing statute), not new rules; still, the underlying ECOA
adverse-action duty is black-letter law.
