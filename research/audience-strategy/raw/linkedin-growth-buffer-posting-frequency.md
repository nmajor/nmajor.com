# LinkedIn Posting Frequency — Buffer study (2M+ posts)

Source: https://buffer.com/resources/how-often-to-post-on-linkedin/
Accessed: 2026-06-19
Method: Buffer analyzed 2 million+ LinkedIn posts from 94,000+ accounts.

## Verbatim findings

Sweet spot: "posting 2 to 5 times weekly on LinkedIn is the sweet spot for improving reach AND engagement without overwhelming your schedule."

Performance tiers (lift vs baseline, per post):
- 2-5 posts weekly: +1,182 impressions per post; +0.23 percentage point engagement lift
- 6-10 posts weekly: +5,001 impressions per post; +0.76 percentage point engagement lift
- 11+ posts weekly: +16,946 impressions per post; +1.40 percentage point engagement lift; 3x more engagements

Frequency ceiling: Buffer's answer to "can you post too much?" — "The short answer: no." Their data indicates posting frequency consistently improves per-post performance across all account sizes without algorithmic suppression.

Content format performance (engagement improvements relative to text posts):
- Images: 72% higher engagement
- Videos: 84% higher engagement
- Carousels: 278% higher than videos; 600% higher than text

Cross-account-size consistency: "Whether you have 500 followers or 50,000, increasing your posting frequency makes your posts perform better relative to your own average."

## NOTE
Buffer's "more is always better" conclusion CONFLICTS with other sources claiming engagement-per-post drops 18-32% beyond 5/week and audience fatigue at 12-15 posts/month. The conflict matters: Buffer measures per-post lift vs the account's own average (relative), while consistency/anti-hype sources emphasize sustainability and audience fatigue. For a part-time anti-hype expert, 2-5/week is the defensible, data-backed range.
