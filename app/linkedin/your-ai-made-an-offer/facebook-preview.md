A car dealership's chatbot offered a customer $27,162.79 for his BMW. That was exactly what he still owed on it, so he'd have walked away clear.

Nobody at the dealership had authorised the number. A human employee had miscommunicated the payoff figure and, as the sales manager put it, "the AI bot ran with that." The bot negotiated, agreed a counter-offer, and booked a meeting to sign.

A rep phoned to revoke it. The dealership only paid up after CBC News called about it.

I wrote about what that says about the systems most companies now have talking to their customers, and about the Air Canada case everyone quotes and almost everyone gets wrong.
