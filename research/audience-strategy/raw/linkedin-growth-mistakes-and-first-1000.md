# Common mistakes + first 1000 followers (founding audience)

Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Common mistakes that kill early growth
Sources: dripify.com/common-linkedin-mistakes, trykondo.com startup-linkedin-sabotage, bupz.com, gptcentral substack
- BROETRY TRAP: dramatic one-sentence-per-line posts that "promise transformation but deliver little substance." May temporarily boost engagement but doesn't build thought leadership or generate qualified leads.
- Engagement bait / treating LinkedIn like other social: overly promotional or irrelevant content that fails to resonate.
- Inconsistent posting, no content plan, ignoring engagement -> reduced visibility/credibility.
- Poor connection strategy: thousands of connections with no real relationship dilutes networking power. Quality > quantity.
- Poor profile/page optimization -> limits search discoverability.
- No clear target audience/buyer persona -> attracts wrong people or fails to engage right ones.
- FIX: educational content addressing target audience's specific challenges/pain points. Insights, how-to guides, case studies, industry analysis — NOT broetry or self-promo.

## First 0->1000 followers (founding audience tactics)
Sources: supergrow.ai, shieldapp.ai, creatoreconomy.so, taplio.com, cleverly.co
- "The first 1,000 followers start with your PROFILE, not your posts." Headline must instantly tell reader who you help and how. People decide in ~3 seconds whether to follow.
- With no audience, best growth lever = find top creators in your niche and REPLY to their posts with valuable insights. "You can get hundreds of new followers from each reply if you do this thoughtfully." Adding value is key; pure self-promo gets you blocked.
- Content: Middle-of-Funnel thought leadership (industry-specific, establishes you as go-to for your ICP) — spend most time here.
- NICHE CLARITY is "the single most underrated growth lever." Most profiles stall at a few thousand because "they're posting for everyone, which means they're actually reaching no one."
- Consistency: book content + networking time into your calendar as a routine.

## ON-BRAND
All of this is strongly on-brand: niche clarity, value-first commenting, educational MOFU content, anti-broetry. This is the spine of a credible-expert playbook. The "hundreds of followers per reply" claim is optimistic/folklore — treat directionally.
