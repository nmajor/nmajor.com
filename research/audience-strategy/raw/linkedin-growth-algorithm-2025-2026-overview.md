## Raw search-result summary (verbatim claims)

Core algorithm shift: LinkedIn's ranking system shifted from rewarding viral reach to "Depth and Authority." The algorithm evaluates content through three core signals:
1. Initial engagement QUALITY within the first 60 minutes
2. Sustained DWELL TIME (how long users actually read content)
3. Creator AUTHENTICITY signals that validate genuine expertise

Quality over volume: An account with 8,000 focused followers can outperform one with 80,000 unfocused ones, because the "Interest Graph" rewards relevance rather than volume. Two to four posts per week is the current sweet spot for most creators. Moving from 1 post/week to 2-4 posts/week adds ~1,234 impressions per post on average.

Content format performance (claims):
- Document posts (PDF carousels) hitting 6.60% engagement rates — highest of any format
- Standard text posts struggle to break 2%
- Videos under 30 seconds achieve 200% higher completion rates than longer formats

Engagement metrics that matter: Algorithm measures how long people actually engage rather than just clicks. "A post someone reads for 30 seconds outperforms one with 50 quick likes." Only 5% of underperforming posts in the first hour recover to reach broader audiences.

What gets PENALIZED:
- Posts with links to external websites see ~60% less reach
- LinkedIn AI detects reciprocal engagement patterns in pods and applies shadowbans without warning; accounts in pods see reach drop from thousands to hundreds overnight
