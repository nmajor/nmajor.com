# Raw capture — CMS 2024 MA final rule + AI/algorithm guidance (fetched 2026-07-07)

## Primary: CMS-4201-F final rule fact sheet
https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/2024-medicare-advantage-and-part-d-final-rule-cms-4201-f
- The **2024 Medicare Advantage and Part D Final Rule (CMS-4201-F)** streamlines prior authorization: "coordinated care plan prior authorization policies may only be used to confirm the presence of diagnoses or other medical criteria and/or ensure that an item or service is medically necessary."
- Requires a minimum **90-day transition period** when an enrollee mid-treatment switches MA plans (no prior auth for the active course of treatment during that window).
- Requires all MA plans to establish a **Utilization Management Committee** to review policies annually.
- Effective for coverage beginning **January 1, 2024.**

## Primary: CMS FAQ on Coverage Criteria & Utilization Management (Feb 6, 2024) — the AI guardrail
CMS FAQ document (HPMS memo / "FAQs Related to Coverage Criteria and Utilization Management Requirements in CMS Final Rule 4201-F"), dated **February 6, 2024.** Verbatim key statements:
- **"An algorithm or software tool predicting the length of a patient's post-acute care stay cannot be the sole grounds for terminating services."** For termination, the patient must no longer meet the level-of-care requirements, determinable only by **re-assessing the individual patient's condition** before issuing the termination notice.
- **"An algorithm or AI cannot be used to solely deny an inpatient admission or downgrade to an observation stay."**
- **"An algorithm that determines coverage based on a larger data set instead of the individual patient's medical history, the physician's recommendations, or clinical notes would not be compliant."**
- MA organizations "must make medical necessity determinations based on the circumstances of specific individuals ... as opposed to relying on an algorithm or software that does not consider individual circumstances."
- CMS also flagged that AI tools must comply with nondiscrimination requirements (Section 1557 of the ACA) — concern they could "exacerbate discrimination and bias."

## What this establishes (for the essay's "human-in-the-loop is a legal requirement" thread)
- Under CMS rules, in Medicare Advantage an algorithm **may assist** but **cannot be the sole basis** for denying/terminating post-acute care or an inpatient admission — an individualized medical-necessity determination is required. This is a **regulator requirement**, effective 2024, and it directly targets exactly the nH-Predict-style length-of-stay tool.
