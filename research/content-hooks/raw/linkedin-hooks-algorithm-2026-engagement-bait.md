## Dwell Time is now the dominant signal (CRITICAL for hooks)
- Dwell time = duration a user spends reading/interacting with a post.
- Algorithm weights it far higher than likes: cited correlation **15.6% vs 1.2%** (dwell vs likes).
- Posts with **61+ seconds dwell time achieve ~15.6% engagement**; posts with 0–3 seconds dwell time only ~1.2%.
- Implication: hooking attention in the first line matters MORE than chasing likes. A hook's job is to buy dwell time, not to bait a reaction.
- "Most LinkedIn reach is now determined within the first 90 minutes after you post."

## "Depth Score" (expertlinked.in)
LinkedIn introduced a "Depth Score" ranking signal measuring actual engagement time, not reaction clicks. Tracks:
- Dwell time, user interaction patterns, saves for later, comment quality, private-message shares.

## Engagement bait is now PENALIZED (key for anti-hype brand)
- "Comment YES if you agree!" and reaction polling: actively detected and deprioritized.
- 2026 update reportedly penalizes engagement bait "by up to 60%."
- Posts with external links penalized "by up to 60%."
- Carousels that previously hit 15,000+ impressions "now barely reach 800 people" — the "swipe for more" format "promising insights while delivering mostly repackaged platitudes" is being downranked.

## Specificity WINS — concrete before/after openers (HIGHLY RELEVANT)
Contrast cited verbatim:
- **Downranked opener:** "Content marketing is changing in 2026"
- **Rewarded opener:** "We spent $23,000 on LinkedIn ads last quarter and generated exactly zero qualified leads"
- **Specificity example:** "I spent $47,000 on a product launch that completely failed. Here's exactly what went wrong."
- Real anecdote: a B2B SaaS marketer posted about a failed launch with exact budget ($47K) and timeline → 10x normal reach + real business conversations; their prior "5 tips for product launches" carousel barely broke 1,000 impressions.

## Reach decline data (context)
- "Average organic post reach has dropped to 8–12% of followers, down from 15–20% a year ago."
- Company page reach: "just 2–4% of followers."
- Personal profiles: "6–8x more reach and engagement than company pages."
- A 2025 study of 300,000+ posts documented average reach declines of 50–65% vs earlier benchmarks.
- LinkedIn VP of Product (Dec 2025 briefing, as reported): 60% of high-engagement Q3 2025 posts used at least one optimization tactic, but user satisfaction scores were declining → motivating the shift toward satisfaction/dwell.

## ANNOTATION (anti-hype alignment — very strong)
- This is the single most important strategic finding: the platform itself is now rewarding the exact thing this brand already does — honest, specific, substantive openers — and PENALIZING clickbait/engagement bait.
- The winning openers ($23K → zero leads; $47K failed launch) are confession/specific-number hooks with NO hype words, NO "it's not X it's Y", NO em-dash drama. They earn the click by being concrete and a little vulnerable.
- AVOID: "Comment YES", "swipe for more", reaction-bait, manufactured curiosity that resolves to platitudes — now actively downranked AND credibility-damaging.
- CAVEAT: the specific percentages (60% penalty, Depth Score naming, VP briefing) come from marketing/SEO blogs, not LinkedIn's official engineering posts — directionally consistent across many sources but treat exact figures as estimates.
