## The verified facts
- **May 5, 2026:** Community Bank (a Pennsylvania regional bank, wholly-owned
  subsidiary of **CB Financial Services, Inc.**, ticker CBFV) discovered an employee
  had used an **unauthorized AI application** to process non-public customer
  information: **names, Social Security numbers, and dates of birth.** (Andrew Hoog
  of Board Cybersecurity called that combination "a full identity-theft starter
  kit.")
- **May 7, 2026:** company determined the incident was **material** (within ~48h of
  discovery).
- **May 11, 2026:** filed a **Form 8-K under Item 1.05** (material cybersecurity
  incident), signed by CEO John H. Montgomery.
- **First of its kind (verbatim, WSGR):** "this was the first-ever [Item] 1.05
  filing triggered by insider AI misuse rather than an external attack." Not a hack —
  an employee's productivity shortcut.
- **The nuance that made it notable:** CB determined the incident material based
  purely on the **sensitivity and volume of data**, while simultaneously stating it
  was **NOT expected to have a material impact on financial condition or operations**
  (no system disruption, no balance-sheet hit). Of 153 cybersecurity 8-Ks in Hoog's
  dataset, CB was unique in disclaiming financial impact while still filing under
  1.05. Lesson: an incident needn't cause financial loss to clear the materiality bar.
- The bank told American Banker it reached the AI vendor in time to keep the data
  from being used to train a model.
- Forbes framing: "Other boards may soon learn that they are only one employee's
  productivity shortcut away from a public filing on a compressed timeline with no
  real opportunity for quiet remediation."

## Why this is a textbook breakout news peg (ties to files 04, 08, 01)
- **FIRST-of-kind / novel** (Vosoughi, file 08; newsjacking, file 04): a genuine
  first — maximally novel AND true.
- **Named, concrete, verifiable** (a real bank, a real filing, a real CEO signature,
  a real dataset of SSNs) — specificity as credibility.
- **High-arousal + moral-emotional** (files 01, 07): anxiety ("this could be my
  company"), surprise ("an 8-K over a chatbot?"), and a fairness/accountability
  frame (ordinary customers' SSNs exposed).
- **Broadly addressable** (file 11): every organization has employees quietly using
  AI tools; every leader fears a compliance surprise they didn't see coming. The
  addressable pool is enormous, far beyond an AI-governance niche.
- **Squarely in Nick's lane** — applied AI, risk, governance for decision-makers — so
  the newsjack is legitimate expertise, not opportunism.
