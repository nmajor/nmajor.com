More than 1,600 court filings have now been caught citing cases the AI invented.

The AI wrote each brief in seconds. Checking it and putting a name on it was still the lawyer's job, and that part never got faster.

This isn't really about lawyers. AI made producing work close to free in every office. It drafts the claim summary or the board report in seconds. But someone still has to check that output and answer for it, and now they have more to check, not less. The expensive step was always the sign-off, and AI didn't touch it.

So the bottleneck in your operation didn't disappear when you added AI. It moved to whoever owns the result.

Before you write off an AI project, look at where the finished-looking work is piling up. If you sped up production and left the person who signs off just as buried, you don't have a failed pilot. You have a faster machine feeding the same jam.
