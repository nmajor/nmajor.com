# NYC Local Law 144 — bias audit for Automated Employment Decision Tools (AEDT)

Captured: 2026-07-07

## Primary: NYC DCWP (the enforcing agency)
URL: https://www.nyc.gov/site/dca/about/automated-employment-decision-tools.page
Rules: https://rules.cityofnewyork.us/rule/automated-employment-decision-tools-updated/

What it requires:
- "Local Law 144 of 2021 ... prohibits employers and employment agencies from using an automated
  employment decision tool unless the tool has been subject to a bias audit within one year of the
  use of the tool, information about the bias audit is publicly available, and certain notices have
  been provided to employees or job candidates."
- Bias audit = **independent** audit testing the AEDT for disparate impact on sex, race/ethnicity
  (and intersectional categories); summary of results must be posted publicly.
- Candidate/employee **notice** required at least **10 business days** before use of the AEDT.
- Effective **January 1, 2023**; enforcement began **July 5, 2023**.
- Enforced by NYC Dept. of Consumer and Worker Protection (DCWP); civil penalties $500-$1,500 per
  violation/per day.

## Assessment (CONFIRMED, in force)
This is a live, in-force US law. NOTE what it does and doesn't do: LL144 mandates an independent
**bias audit + disclosure + notice** for AI hiring tools. It does NOT itself mandate a human
decision-maker on each hire. So it's a strong "regulator now imposes AI guardrails on a
consequential decision about a person (employment)" fact, but it is an *audit/transparency*
requirement, not strictly a *human-in-the-loop* requirement. Caveat for honesty: enforcement has
been widely reported as light/low-volume (NY State Comptroller and others have flagged weak
compliance) — cite it as "the first US algorithmic-hiring audit mandate," not as vigorously enforced.
