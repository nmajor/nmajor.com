# Campaign Monitor — 7 Data-Backed Ways to Write Email Subject Lines

Source: https://www.campaignmonitor.com/blog/email-marketing/7-data-backed-ways-to-write-email-subject-lines-that-boost-open-rates/
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Open Rate Statistics
- Emoji effect: "Brands that are using emojis have seen a 56% increase in their unique open rates"
- Numbers impact: "email open and reply rates are higher when a number is present in the subject line" (based on analysis of 115 million emails)
- Preheader addition: "adding emails with preheaders and subject lines can boost open rates by 7%"

## Optimal Length Recommendations
- Character length: "17-24 characters long are most likely to boost your email open rates"
- Word count: "Use 3-5 words in your subject line to boost your email open rates"

## Formatting Guidance
- Title case effectiveness: Subject lines in title case are "most effective at boosting your open and reply rates" due to perceived authority
- Emoji quantity: "using 1-4 emojis in your subject line will boost your email open rates"

## Mobile Consideration
- "50% of all emails are opened on mobile devices" requiring shorter character lengths for readability

## Preheader Best Practices
- Ideal length: "85-100 characters"
- Include personalization (customer names) and strong action verbs

## Note
The article references "20 studies" backing the subject line recommendations.

## ANALYST NOTE (for brand fit)
- Number-in-subject and short length guidance are honest/credible-friendly.
- Emoji + title-case "authority" claims conflict with anti-hype, plain-language voice (manufactured emphasis). Treat emoji advice as AVOID for this brand.
