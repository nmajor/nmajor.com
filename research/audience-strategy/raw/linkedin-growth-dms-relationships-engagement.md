# Engagement strategy, DMs & relationship building (B2B)

Accessed: 2026-06-19

## The 80/20 engagement rule
Sources: blog.socialhp.com (80/20 rule), lagrowthmachine.com, linkboost, medium relationship-first
- "80/20 Engagement Rule: spend 80% of your LinkedIn time engaging with others' content and only 20% creating your own."
- A thoughtful comment on a popular post can get more visibility than your own posts.
- Commenting on larger accounts puts your name/profile in front of their audience -> profile visits + follow requests.

## "Engage before you post" / warming the feed
Source: blog.socialhp.com, authoredup, socialrails
- Engaging in the hour BEFORE posting makes your own content more likely to appear in those people's feeds ("warming the feed").
- Spend 15-30 min commenting on niche posts before AND after you publish. Comments in the first hour of a post's life are most impactful (helps tip the algo's decision to push wider).
- Comments over 50 words get significantly more engagement (show effort). Add value: share experience, offer a different perspective, ask a thoughtful follow-up. Avoid empty "Great post!"

## DMs / converting connections to clients (relationship-first)
Sources: medium relationship-first (Paula Graziani), linkboost convert-connections, devcommx, leannecalderwood
- "A LinkedIn DM sequence is not a shorter cold email but a conversation opener: first message references a specific signal, asks ONE question, contains ZERO pitch."
- Content warms the relationship before the first message: by the time you DM, the prospect already knows you from your feed/comments.
- Trigger-based outreach: reach out when prospect comments on a post, downloads a lead magnet, or announces a milestone.
- Relationship-first = turning connections into partnerships, not dropping a pitch in the inbox.
- B2B proof: 73% of B2B buyers prefer sales pros referred by someone they know; 87% have favorable impression of a salesperson introduced via their network.

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID
- ON-BRAND: 80/20 (engage more than you post — especially crucial from zero), value-adding comments on peers/leaders, warming relationships via genuine content + comments, zero-pitch conversational DMs.
- AVOID: automated DM sequences/profile-view-trigger tricks, "spray" connection requests, cold pitch DMs, pods. Anything automated/manipulative undermines a credibility brand.
