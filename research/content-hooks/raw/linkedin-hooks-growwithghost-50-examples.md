## 7 Hook Categories
1. **Contrarian Hook** - Challenges industry conventional wisdom
2. **Data Hook** - Uses specific numbers/statistics
3. **Story Hook** - Narrative-driven with transformation
4. **Question Hook** - Engages reader's brain through inquiry
5. **Confession Hook** - Shows vulnerability and relatability
6. **List Hook** - Promises organized, digestible information
7. **Challenge Hook** - Calls out behaviors or provokes thinking

## Key Mechanics (verbatim/near-verbatim)
- **Length:** "5-15 words" is optimal for mobile consumption
- **Timing:** Contrarian hooks perform better during peak business hours; story hooks work during lunch breaks
- **Algorithm Focus:** Posts judged on "dwell time" rather than clicks alone. Strong hooks create a "curiosity gap"
- **Testing:** Monitor engagement quality (comments/shares) within first hour; track profile views and connection requests
- **Performance Data claim:** Posts with strong hooks generate "340% more engagement" versus generic openings
- Odd numbers outperform even in list hooks
- Avoid yes/no questions; use ones requiring deeper consideration
- Mobile-first perspective: hooks must stop scrollers immediately
- "Curiosity gap" psychology drives effectiveness

## ANNOTATION (subagent note for parsing)
- Many "Data" examples cite unsourced/round percentages — these are the kind of unverifiable stat hooks that would read as hype/clickbait to a skeptical audience UNLESS backed by the writer's OWN real data.
- "The 7-word phrase that ends every objection" / "Number 4 cost me the most" style = curiosity-gap baiting; clickbait-leaning, AVOID for anti-hype brand.
- Contrarian "It's not X, it's Y" structure (#1, #4, #6, #10) is EXACTLY the banned rhetorical pattern for this brand.
- The 340% figure is repeated across many SEO blogs with no primary source — treat as marketing folklore, not evidence.
