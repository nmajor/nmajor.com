# Newsletter Glue — Newsletter Introduction Examples (opening-line techniques)

Source: https://blog.newsletterglue.com/grow/newsletter-introduction-examples/
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Techniques (with how each works)
1. Clear Benefit Statement — immediately state what the newsletter covers and who it's for. Second line tells subscribers who it's for and the benefit.
2. Direct Main Point — lead with the most important info without delay. (Jared) "immediately dives into the main point of the email so that you know exactly what the rest of the email will be about."
3. Curiosity Hook — create intrigue by mentioning something special is coming (Simon: hints at a present that "has finally arrived"... "something special just for his subscribers").
4. Personal Story Opening — begin with relatable human details before pivoting to content (Viktor opens with weather in Armenia and his vacation, building relationship before UI tips).
5. Consistent Summary Line — a repeating one-liner that sets expectations. Example: "Brain Food is a weekly newsletter full of timeless insights."

## ANALYST NOTE
Brand-aligned picks: "Direct Main Point" and "Clear Benefit Statement" — tell the reader what this is and why it matters fast, which respects busy operators' time. "Personal Story" works only if short and tied to the point (the brand bans rambling/hype, so keep anecdotes tight and relevant). "Consistent Summary Line" (cf. Brain Food, Morning Brew editor's note) is a low-risk, repeatable, honest structure.
