# Raw search results — batch 1 (2026-07-13)

## Institute 10-K report (WebFetch of appliedartificialintelligence.org)
- Insurance highest 82.9%; tech 77.4%; transport/logistics 71%; telecom/media 66%; retail 55%; manufacturing 55%; healthcare 54%; real estate 49%; energy 48%; banks/lenders LOWEST 22.8%.
- Methodology caveat (verbatim): "It measures disclosure. A 10-K is what a company chose to tell investors under legal constraint, graded for how concrete the AI language is. It is not an audit of what companies run."
- Spot-check: 12 production-tier filers; SEVEN had AI results in investor decks/news releases that never appeared in 10-Ks → underreporting of deployment.
- Explanation offered: "Insurance's rate may be raised by state disclosure expectations" (authors did NOT control for this). No explanation given for banks' low rate.
- Sample: 2,112 coded companies across 4,696 traditional-sector 10-K filers in 2025.

## Insurance AI adoption survey data
- ~90% of insurers evaluating or implementing AI by 2025; ~three-quarters of US carriers use AI in claims & underwriting.
- Conning 2025 survey: 90% of insurers somewhere on genAI journey; 55% early or full deployment; machine learning 74% adoption.
- NAIC by line: 92% health insurers, 88% auto, 70% home, 58% life report current/planned AI use.
- Projection: by 2026 ~80% of insurers deploying AI in >=1 core function; AI automating 50-60% of claims, cutting processing costs 25-40%.
- Only 7% of insurers have brought AI systems to scale.
- Sources: BCG "Insurance Leads in AI Adoption. Now It's Time to Scale" (2025); Conning 2025 survey (conning.com/about-us/news/ir-pr---ai-survey-2025); NAIC AI topics (content.naic.org/insurance-topics/artificial-intelligence); Insurance Business Mag survey.

## Banks — regulatory caution / disclosure
- SR 11-7 (Fed model risk mgmt): all models incl AI/ML must be understood, tested, monitored; black-box opacity won't excuse compliance failure.
- Fair lending: ECOA + Fair Housing Act prohibit discrimination even if unintentional; biased training data → violations. OCC examiners analyze AI outputs for disparate impact.
- No US regulator has issued AI-specific rule for banks; FFIEC agencies say existing model-risk/third-party/fair-lending/BSA-AML guidance applies, AI in scope.
- Banks cautious due to uncertainty on accountability, ethics, privacy, fairness, transparency, explainability — esp consumer apps. Fear of enforcement/adverse supervisory outcomes delays adoption. Months-long model risk reviews before deploying even low-risk AI.
- Sources: BIS FSI Insights No 63 (bis.org/fsi/publ/insights63.pdf); Bank Policy Institute "The Overlooked Risk in Bank AI Adoption: Regulatory Inaction" (bpi.com); American Banker opinion; Deloitte; TALG law.

## JPMorgan — actual bank AI deployment (contrast to low disclosure)
- 500+ active AI use cases in production. AML false positives cut 95%. LLM Suite used daily by 230,000+ employees (American Banker 2025 Innovation of the Year).
- Named: COiN (legal analysis), LOXM (trade execution), IndexGPT (investment strategies), EVEE Q&A call center assistant, AI coding assistant.
- Jamie Dimon: $2bn operational savings.
- Sources: emerj.com/artificial-intelligence-at-jpmorgan-chase; digitaldefynd JP Morgan case study; klover.ai.

## Insurance regulation (why disclosure high)
- NAIC 2023 Model Bulletin: Use of AI Systems by Insurers — governance, documentation, audit; adopted by many states.
- Colorado SB 205 / C.R.S. §10-3-1104.9: prohibits external data/predictive models causing unfair discrimination; requires quantitative bias/disparate-impact testing; annual reporting to Division; senior-mgmt accountability. Expanded Oct 15 2025 to private passenger auto & health plans.
- CA, CO, NY each have distinct algorithmic-tool regimes. NAIC bulletin + CO SB205 + NY Circular Letter all require board/senior-mgmt AI accountability.
- Sources: Troutman/Locke Lord "State Insurance Regulation: A Year in Review"; Fenwick; WaterStreet; NAIC model bulletin PDF; S&P Global (NAIC divided on model law/disclosure standard, Oct 2025).
