# Named practitioners: Jasmin Alic & Lara Acosta

Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Jasmin Alic ("Jay Clouse"? no — Jasmin Alić, ghostwriter/creator)
Sources: thefutur.com, getcreator.io/learn/how-jasmin-ali-writes-on-linkedin, thelinkedinsecrets.com, scribd "27 Proven LinkedIn Writing Tips by Jasmin Alic"

- First lines: LinkedIn shows only ~3 lines in feed preview. These lines must carry the entire value proposition; fail there and no one reads the rest.
- Hook: one line, strategic white space, second line works in harmony with the first; curiosity-gap hook that delays payoff, then numbered scannable lists.
- Writing framework: talk like a human not a robot; "ask before you teach" (start with a problem the audience faces); write as a conversation, include the audience, ask questions, end with a gentle nudge for replies.
- ENGAGEMENT IS THE MAIN EVENT: treats comments as primary, not afterthought. Generates 1,000+ comments per post via structured engagement time, strategic questions, and "pin commenting" (pinning a value-add comment to drive deeper discussion).
- Stats: posts 15x/week; averages 3,662 engagements/post; conversation index 39.6% (≈4 in 10 engagements are comments, not likes).

## Lara Acosta (laraacostar)
Sources: creatorscience podcast #241, snipd #154, medium masterclass, her LinkedIn posts

- Profile: 20M+ impressions, 300k+ followers, grew from 0 to 107k (and beyond), called #1 female creator on LinkedIn; coached 500+ clients. Went 0 -> 6/7-figure entrepreneur on personal brand.
- SLAY framework: Story, Lesson, Advice, (Y) — begin with a personal story to make content relatable, then lesson + advice.
- Her own "how to build a personal brand from 0-107k" advice (verbatim-ish from her post):
  1. Educate others on ONE specific skill
  2. Write like you think, not like you talk
  3. Use comments to grow (comment on others' posts)
- Focus on ONE platform until message and niche are validated; expand only after solidifying LinkedIn.
- "Show, Don't Tell" framework: transformation, struggles, journey, win; avoid too much context.
- Emphasizes authentic storytelling and "genuine connections over inflated engagement metrics."
- Engagement: comment on competitors'/peers' posts to reach their audience.

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID
- ON-BRAND: Lara's "educate on one specific skill," "genuine connections over inflated metrics," validate niche; Jasmin's "ask before you teach," human voice, comments as the real conversation.
- WATCH: 15 posts/week (Jasmin) is full-time creator volume, NOT viable or advisable part-time, and conflicts with anti-fatigue data. Heavy "personal story" openings can drift into manufactured-vulnerability clickbait if not genuine — use real stories only.
