## Source: Search synthesis (Oktopost, usevisuals, Expandi)
URLs: https://www.oktopost.com/blog/linkedin-carousel-pdf-best-practices/ ; https://usevisuals.com/blog/linkedin-carousel-post-tips ; https://expandi.io/blog/linkedin-carousel/

- Native multi-image carousels were DISCONTINUED December 2023. Carousels now = **document posts (multi-page PDF upload)** that render as swipeable slides. This workaround remains "one of the most effective organic formats" in 2026.
- Document posts drive higher dwell time than static single-image posts (positive algorithm signal → higher reach).
- Slide count: most high-performing B2B carousels use **5–15 slides**; <5 may not justify the swipe; >15 risks losing the reader. Sweet spot **3–10**. 3–5 = quick tips/high-level; 6–10 = deeper story/case study.
- Format spec note: square (1080x1080) or portrait (1080x1350) PDF; mobile-first; big readable text.

## How to turn an article into a carousel (synthesized best practice)
- Slide 1 = hook (the contrarian/value promise, mirror the post hook).
- Slides 2–N = one point per slide (map to the article's 3–7 distinct points).
- Penultimate slide = recap/summary.
- Final slide = soft CTA (subscribe to newsletter for the full breakdown).
- A carousel is the "Direct"/"Micro-framework" slice type from the ADAPT/Flywheel frameworks.

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID (carousels)
- ON-BRAND: dense, genuinely useful slide decks (frameworks, checklists, decision criteria) that an enterprise decision-maker would save. Save/share = strong honest signal.
- AVOID: "swipe →" engagement-bait with no payoff; padding to hit slide counts; withholding the actual answer to the last slide as a bait.
