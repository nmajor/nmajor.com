## SOURCE: Search synthesis — LinkedIn DM strategy that books calls without spam
Sources: b2bsalesscientist Substack "5 LinkedIn DM Templates"; salesrobot.co DM masterclass; shabbirjasden Substack "How I Book Clients Through LinkedIn DMs in 5 Steps"; Jasmin Alic (alicjasmin) LinkedIn post on DMs.
Accessed: 2026-06-19

Core principles (ON-BRAND):
- "The fastest way to get ignored on LinkedIn is to treat your first message like a sales call." Instead, open by sharing an actionable insight/resource tailored to recipient's role/industry -> positions you as helpful, not transactional.
- "Warm reachout" = approach people who already know you; "give so much value that they buy from you rather than you selling to them."
- Personalize the first message so it doesn't sound like a bot.
- 3-4 message sequence works best; more risks annoying; each message must add something new/valuable.
- Low-friction CTA example (VERBATIM): "I am researching this topic, and I would love your opinion. Would you have 5-10 minutes to answer a few questions?" — addresses their need to feel like experts, non-threatening.
- Only offer further help / a deal AFTER value delivered and trust established.

AVOID (sleazy): listing your services in the first DM; pitch-first; bot-templated mass messages; >4 messages.

## SOURCE: Search synthesis — productized consulting pricing & packaging
Sources: ManyRequests "Productized Consulting 2026"; QuantumByte "Productizing Consulting examples + pricing"; Wayfront "13 productized service examples"; Melisa Liberman "Productized Consulting 101"; Consulting Success productization.
Accessed: 2026-06-19

Definition: productized service = fixed price, fixed scope, standardized workflow. Pricing should be VALUE-based (target audience, perceived value, competitor pricing).

Three-tier framework (most successful implementations):
- Diagnostic services that identify problems: $2,000-$10,000  <-- maps to "AI readiness assessment / audit" entry offer
- Implementation packages that solve specific challenges: $10,000-$50,000
- Optimization offerings (improve existing systems): $3,000-$15,000 / month

Broader ranges:
- One-off projects: $500-$5,000
- Monthly retainers: $1,500-$10,000
- Unlimited-model subscriptions: $3,000-$15,000 / month

Fixed-scope examples:
- SEO consultant stopped selling "SEO strategy", started selling monthly audit + prioritized fixes + roadmap. Clients don't question value because deliverables clear, process consistent, results trackable.
- HR consultant: fixed scope = assessment with up to 25 participants; deliverables = synthesize interview/survey results into exec summary + detailed report with recommendations.

Principle: every tier covers real costs + healthy margin + reflects client value; fixed scope protects margins (anti scope-creep).

DESIGN IMPLICATION for Nick: a paid "AI Readiness / Workflow Automation Audit" (diagnostic, $2k-$10k) is the natural first paid step from a free assessment lead magnet -> escalates to implementation -> retainer/optimization.

## SOURCE: Search synthesis — personal brand vs company page (B2B founder)
Sources: digitalapplied.com "Personal vs Company Pages 8x"; runmark.net "Why People Outperform Company Pages"; Grou "LinkedIn page vs profile B2B"; meet-lea.com reach 2026; heysid.com "Personal Brand vs Business Brand B2B 2026".
Accessed: 2026-06-19

Performance gap: personal profiles outperform company pages by 5-8x on engagement. Algorithm distributes personal content via social graph + interest signals; company page content mostly reaches existing followers only.

Why personal wins: "people trust people — not logos." More likely to trust a founder/consultant/operator sharing useful ideas than a company page posting polished updates.
- Posts triggering 3+ commenters in first 60 min get ~5.2x reach amplification; personal profiles structurally win this window (peers comment on people, not brand posts).

Strategic recommendation (KEY for institute + Nick relationship):
- NOT mutually exclusive. Personal profiles = primary organic investment / growth channel; company page = irreplaceable for advertising, hiring, brand credibility.
- "The most effective LinkedIn strategies do not choose between formats — they assign each format its optimal role and build bridges between them through employee advocacy and paid amplification."

DESIGN IMPLICATION: Nick (personal) is the audience-growth + selling engine; appliedartificialintelligence.org (institute) provides credibility, hosts the lead magnets/assessments/proposals, and is the entity that gets contracted. Personal posts -> institute assets -> booked calls.
