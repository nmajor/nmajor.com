# MarTech — Beyond Clickbait: Avoid Deception in Email Subject Lines

Source: https://martech.org/beyond-clickbait-avoid-deception-in-email-subject-lines/
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Core Tension
"Email marketers need to lean into their subject lines going forward, but must avoid deception at all costs." With AI replacing preheader copy, subject lines carry more persuasive burden, but pressure doesn't justify crossing ethical lines.

## Why Deception Damages Trust
- Research cited: "30.4% of subscribers would opt out of emails whose subject lines didn't line up with the message content." (2022 Gartner study per related coverage)
- Subscribers may ignore emails or report as spam, jeopardizing "the financial success of your email program and can even affect deliverability."

## Five Categories of Deceptive Tactics (AVOID)
1. Fake abandonment: "RE: Your pending order"
2. Fake forwards: "FWD: $100 Credit"
3. Fake errors: "FWD: Code Error"
4. False urgency resends: "Did you forget something?"
5. Layered deception: "Fw: 50% OFF Code you requested"

## Trust Principle
"Customer trust is hard to win and easy to lose. The inbox can be your reliable ally in the battle."
Remedy: "Always be sure it supports your campaign message."

## ANALYST NOTE
Directly supports the brand's anti-clickbait email rule. The fake "RE:"/"FWD:" tactics are exactly what reads as manipulative to skeptical CTOs/operators. Strong AVOID list.
