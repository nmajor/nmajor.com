# Content mix / pillars, profile optimization, 30/60/90 days

Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Content pillars & mix (B2B expert)
Sources: stackmatix, socialmeep, lagrowthmachine, nationalsearchgroup, influenceflow, Justin Welsh (lanes)
- Define 3-5 content pillars = recurring themes you "own." Common set: Core Expertise (primary skill/service), Industry Insights (trends/news/analysis), Personal Lessons (stories from experience), Thought Leadership (contrarian takes, predictions, frameworks).
- Justin Welsh: pick 3-4 "content lanes" you talk about regularly — gives direction, makes idea generation easier.
- Mix ratios cited:
  - 3:2:1 — 3 educate, 2 personal story/experience, 1 promote what you offer.
  - 5:3:2 — 10-post cycle: 5 curated, 3 original, 2 personal.
- Educational content (frameworks, data breakdowns, how-tos) consistently outperforms promotional for B2B lead gen. Personal stories tied to business lessons + transparent metric-sharing + posts ending with a direct question drive the most meaningful engagement from target accounts.
- B2B audience wants: 24% educational product info; 24% updates from company leadership. 76% of B2B marketers say LinkedIn is most effective channel for thought leadership.

## Profile optimization (foundation before posting)
Sources: growleads, valuehits, wavecnct, sociabble, creatoreconomy, Ruben Hassid
- Headline appears everywhere (search, requests, comments, post authorship, messages) and carries more algorithmic weight than any other field. 220-char limit; headlines using 100+ chars get significantly more search impressions. Should instantly say who you help + how (3-sec follow decision).
- Banner = "visual headline," first thing after photo; 70% leave it blank (missed opportunity). Highlight target user's desired outcome.
- Featured section = prime real estate above the fold; pin best work (case studies, signature posts, lead magnet).
- About: compelling, explains why to follow.
- Profile pic (Ruben): smile more than feels natural; "own a color"; zoom so face fills frame.
- Claimed result: full rebuild raised visitor-to-conversation 0.8% -> 2.9% over 90 days; profile views 5-10x within 30 days; each change ~10-15 min. (Treat the precise numbers as vendor claims/folklore.)

## First 30/60/90 days from zero (synthesis)
- Days 0-30: Optimize profile completely (headline, banner, about, featured), enable creator mode. Lock niche + ICP-of-one. Pick 3-4 content pillars. Build "engage list" (peers, leaders, prospects). Spend MOST time commenting (80/20) to seed founding audience. Start posting 2-4x/week. Don't over-analyze yet.
- Days 30-60: Keep cadence; analyze 2-4 weeks of data; double down on resonant pillars/formats; test documents/carousels (highest engagement, underused at ~4.88%). Deepen relationships via DMs (zero-pitch conversation openers). Consider launching a newsletter (bypasses feed algorithm).
- Days 60-90: Consistency compounds; expect meaningful inbound around 3-6 months. Maintain 80/20 engage:post. Reuse top posts; build owned audience (newsletter). Begin trigger-based relationship outreach to prospects who engage.

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID
- ON-BRAND: educational/framework-heavy mix (3:2:1 leaning educational), real personal lessons tied to business, transparent (share what worked AND didn't), niche clarity, profile that states who-you-help. Perfect for an honest applied-AI expert.
- AVOID: promotion-heavy mix; manufactured vulnerability stories; vanity profile claims; "Top Voice via collaborative articles" is low-value busywork; engagement-bait CTAs.
- FOLKLORE FLAGS: "70% leave banner blank," "5-10x views in 30 days," "0.8%->2.9%," "100+ char headlines get more impressions" — vendor/blog claims, no traceable primary source. Directional only.
