# LinkedIn Algorithm 2026 — Dataslayer (cites Richard van der Blom)

Source: https://www.dataslayer.ai/blog/linkedin-algorithm-february-2026-whats-working-now
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Verbatim data

Overall performance decline (cited to Richard van der Blom's Algorithm Insights 2025 Report):
- Views down 50%
- Engagement down 25%
- Follower growth down 59% (vs 2025)

Format performance benchmarks:
- Document posts (PDF carousels): 6.60% average engagement (highest format). "278% more engagement than video," "596% more than text-only posts." Dataslayer internal testing: 40.5% engagement vs 10.7% for other formats.
- Native video (30-90 sec): 5.60% average engagement. Video views grew 36% YoY. Video creation growing 2x faster than other formats.
- Text-only posts: 2.00% average engagement.
- Posts with external links: ~60% less reach than identical posts without links. "Link in first comment workaround also penalized as of early 2026."

Algorithm priorities:
- Dwell time over likes: "The algorithm measures how long people actually engage with your content, not just whether they clicked."
- First 60 minutes critical: "Only 5% of underperforming posts in the first hour recover to reach broader audiences."

Posting cadence:
- Company pages: 3-5 posts/week
- Personal profiles: daily acceptable if quality maintained
- "One valuable post per week consistently outperforms five forgettable ones"

LinkedIn newsletters: "bypass the feed algorithm entirely" — delivered directly via push notification and email, avoiding reach limitations.

## NOTE
This is the most-cited primary-ish source: Richard van der Blom's annual "Algorithm Insights" report (he surveys/analyzes large sample of posts). Worth treating his numbers as the closest thing to authoritative. The decline numbers (views -50%, etc.) are heavily repeated and trace to his report.
