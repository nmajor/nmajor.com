## 7 Lead-In Techniques (with verbatim examples)

1. The Summary Lead — deliver key facts upfront for news/announcements.
   Example: "Google is sunsetting third-party cookies in 2025, a shift that could upend how 60% of ad spend is tracked online."

2. The Anecdotal Lead — humanize complexity through storytelling.
   Guidance: "1 detail > 5 generalizations" — specificity beats broad statements.

3. The Scene Setter — atmospheric entry; establish mood and pacing, but the cinematic opening must connect to substantive material.

4. The Question Lead — trigger curiosity and tension.
   Example: "Your email open rates are tanking. Is it your subject line, or your timing?"
   Guidance: Avoid generic questions with obvious answers; the question must expose tension or spark genuine curiosity.

5. The Observational Lead — frame bigger-picture insights.
   Guidance: "Don't mistake vagueness for insight" — observations must feel fresh, true, or gutsy, not obvious.

6. The First-Person Lead — build connection through personal narrative.
   Guidance: Story must connect to your larger point; avoid oversharing without takeaway.

7. The Zinger — short, sharp opening.
   Example: "Your landing page reads like a hostage negotiation."
   Guidance: "Substance must follow; zingers without supporting material feel like clickbait."

## ANALYST NOTE
Brand-aligned: summary lead, observational lead, and first-person lead all work for skeptical operators. Key recurring caveat across all 7: the opener must be backed by real substance — same anti-clickbait principle applied to the body, not just the subject line. "1 detail > 5 generalizations" maps perfectly to the brand's specificity rule.
