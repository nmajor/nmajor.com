# nH Predict — the OLDER, non-LLM tool (distinct from the 2026 generative push)

## What nH Predict is (verbatim)
Wikipedia: "nH Predict is a computer program developed by naviHealth that implements an algorithm that has allegedly been used by health insurance companies including United Healthcare and Humana to automatically deny coverage to patients. It is reported to work by cross-correlating patient health records with those of other patients. It is estimated that such software automates away 50-75% of human labor..."

Earlier reporting (STAT/AIAAIC) on mechanism: factors in diagnosis, age, living situation, physical function to MATCH a patient against a database of ~6 million prior patients, then predicts mobility/cognition, estimated length of stay, and discharge date.

Senate PSI report context: "In December of 2022, a UnitedHealthcare working group met to explore how to use AI and 'machine learning' to predict which denials of post-acute care cases were likely to be appealed."

## HONESTY-CRITICAL CONCLUSION on nH Predict
- nH Predict is a TRADITIONAL PREDICTIVE / MACHINE-LEARNING (patient-matching / correlation) model. It is NOT a large language model and NOT generative AI. It predates the "advent of generative AI" that Dadlani cites for the 2026 push.
- The lawsuit is about nH Predict, NOT about Avery / Optum Real / Digital Auth Complete / PreCheck MyScript. Conflating the two would be inaccurate.

## Lawsuit status & the two theories (verbatim from Healthcare Finance News)
Case: Estate of Gene B. Lokken et al. v. UnitedHealth Group, Inc. et al. (D. Minn., filed Nov 2023).
- Plaintiffs: nH Predict "would sometimes supersede physician judgement, and has a 90% error rate, meaning nine of 10 appealed denials were ultimately reversed." (Note: "90% error rate" = share of APPEALED denials reversed, not all denials.)
- STAT investigation (cited in suit): UNH "pressured employees to use the algorithm to issue payment denials," "setting a goal for employees to keep patient rehabilitation stays within 1% of the length of stay predicted by nH Predict."
- Plaintiffs' financial theory (the best-sourced "savings = avoided payouts" argument): "The fraudulent scheme affords defendants a clear financial windfall in the form of policy premiums without having to pay for promised care."
- Two remaining claims: breach of contract; breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

## UnitedHealth's on-record REBUTTAL (verbatim, important for fairness)
"nH Predict is not used to make coverage decisions, but instead is a 'guide to help us inform providers, families and other caregivers about what sort of assistance and care the patient may need both in the facility and after returning home.'" The company added that "coverage decisions are based on the terms of the members' health plans and on criteria laid out by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services."
