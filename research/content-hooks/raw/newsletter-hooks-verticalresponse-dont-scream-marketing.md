# VerticalResponse — Subject Lines That Don't Scream Marketing (15 Examples)

Source: https://verticalresponse.com/blog/subject-lines-that-dont-scream-marketing-email/
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Six Core Principles
1. Keep it short: 3-7 words, under 50 characters. "Research shows subject lines under 50 characters achieve 56% open rates."
2. Use sentence case: "quick question about your order" not Title Case or ALL CAPS.
3. Avoid promotional language: skip "Free," "Act now," excessive punctuation (trigger spam filters).
4. Focus on the recipient: lead with what matters to the reader (their project/renewal), not your company name.
5. Match your promise: if subject says "quick question," deliver an actual quick question, not a sales pitch.
6. Maintain consistent voice: build trust through recognizable, authentic brand communication.

## 15 Example Subject Lines (verbatim, sentence case)
Quick Check-In:
- "quick check-in on your March order"
- "touching base re: your renewal"
- "still good for Thursday?"
- "checking in on the proposal"

FYI/Update:
- "fyi on your Q2 report"
- "small update on your booking"
- "note on your subscription settings"

Specific Question:
- "one question about your onboarding"
- "ok to share this with finance?"
- "how did the webinar go?"

Past Interaction:
- "following up on our 5/14 call"
- "about your request last week"

Document/Asset:
- "your 2025 pricing summary"
- "draft proposal attached"

Low-Pressure Close:
- "ok if we pause this for now?"
- "leaving this with you"

## Why They Work
"These patterns succeed because they mimic authentic professional communication—direct, specific, and free from hype. They signal a real person wrote them, not an automation tool."

## ANALYST NOTE
This is the single most brand-aligned source: sentence case, plain, specific, "reads like a person wrote it." These are 1:1 with the brand's email rule. Note: these are sales/relationship examples; for a newsletter, adapt the same tone to content (e.g., "what we got wrong about RAG").
