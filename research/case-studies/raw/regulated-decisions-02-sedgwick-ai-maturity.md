# Sedgwick — insurer AI maturity gap (better source than the Decerto vendor page)

## Primary trade-press source: Insurance Journal
URL: https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/national/2026/03/10/861186.htm
Captured: 2026-07-07

Report identity:
- Report title: "Future-ready property claims: Leveraging technology and AI for a strategic advantage"
- Publisher: **Sedgwick** (global claims administrator / TPA) — this is Sedgwick's own report,
  so the numbers ORIGINATE with an interested industry party, but are reported by independent
  trade press (Insurance Journal, Risk & Insurance, Insurance Business). Sample size /
  methodology were NOT disclosed in the coverage.
- Date: March 2026 (report launched ~March 10, 2026).

Figures (as reported by Insurance Journal, verbatim/near-verbatim):
- "between 58% and 82% of insurers use AI tools in their operations"
- "just 12% [say] they have fully mature AI capabilities"
- "only 7% say they have achieved scalable AI success"
- "82% of carriers use AI for routine tasks (data extraction, automated customer interactions)"
- "75% of claims professionals believe AI needs human oversight"

- Frames the gap as carriers stuck in "pilot purgatory" — AI adopted widely but failing to

## Assessment
The "58-82% use AI / 12% mature / 7% scaled" figures are CONFIRMED as accurately quoted from
the Sedgwick report (multiple independent trade outlets carry the identical numbers) — so the
Decerto vendor page was faithfully repeating Sedgwick, not inventing. BUT the numbers are still
a vendor/industry-report statistic (Sedgwick is a claims TPA with a commercial interest in the
"AI isn't mature yet, come to us" narrative), no public methodology/sample. Tier: REPORTED, not
CONFIRMED-primary. Cite as "Sedgwick's 2026 property-claims report (via Insurance Journal)."
