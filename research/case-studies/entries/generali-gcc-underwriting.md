## What they did
Generali's Global Corporate & Commercial arm replaced a fragmented IT landscape across 25
countries with a single cloud-native SaaS platform (AGORA, built on Microsoft Dynamics 365, Power
Platform, and SharePoint) and added a Gen AI assistant that answers platform questions, automates
routine tasks, and handles service requests. The stated aim was to let underwriters complete
pre-bind underwriting steps faster. Delivered with Capgemini.

## The numbers
- Pre-bind underwriting activities: 80% faster (vendor-reported).
- Software licensing cost: down 60% (vendor-reported, this is platform consolidation, not AI).
- Support / maintenance staffing cost: down 75% (vendor-reported).
- Service desk tickets: down 75% in the first three months post-launch (vendor-reported).
- 1.9 TB of documents migrated to SharePoint.
- Underwriter productivity "significantly increased" with no percentage given.

## What broke / the caveats
This is a Capgemini-authored client story, i.e. marketing, and should be read as such. The headline
"80% faster" measures one process step (pre-bind activity completion), not end-to-end underwriting,
turnaround, or any loss/combined-ratio outcome. Most of the other savings (licensing, support,
tickets) are from consolidating onto one Microsoft SaaS platform, which would have happened with or
without Gen AI; the AI assistant is one feature inside that migration, and its specific contribution
is not isolated. No baseline figures, no absolute dollars, no timeline.

## Why it matters for our beat
A large multinational insurer's commercial line standardizing 25 countries onto one platform and
bolting a Gen AI assistant onto underwriting workflow. Useful as a data point on what "Gen AI in
underwriting" often actually is in 2026: a thin assistant layer on top of a much bigger, conventional
software-consolidation program.

## Post angles
- The honest read of vendor case studies: a "80% faster" headline that, unpacked, is mostly a SaaS

- Feeds the insurance-underwriting synthesis (AIG, Zurich/Cytora, Allianz) as the "assistant layer"
