## Justin Welsh — "My Writing Process for 162.597M+ Impressions"
Source URL: https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/my-writing-process-for-162-597m-impressions-on-linkedin

Three-step content structure:
1. **The "Meat"** — core teaching/advice.
2. **The "Trailer"** — opening section designed to stop scrolling.
3. **Summary + CTC (call-to-conversation)** — recap + engagement driver.

Hook principles:
- Understand audience first: "What do they care about?"
- Trailer formula, two jobs:
  1. "Break the scroll pattern with the first line."
  2. "Make each line intriguing enough to get the reader to the next one." (= Sugarman slippery slide)

Hook example (verbatim):
- Opening: "I generated $1.5M in course sales in 2022 using a simple framework" — uses a concrete revenue figure (his OWN real number).
- The line positioned right before "see more": "Here are 2 big learnings and some advice:" — the commitment moment.

Welsh stats cited: 472.9M+ views, 7.1M reactions, ~2M comments; $12M+ profit at 90% margins over six years.
Note: Welsh's hooks succeed by being "extremely clear about the topic and benefit, while adding a twist that sets up curiosity." Clarity + specificity over cleverness.

## Jasmin Alić — "LinkedIn is literally telling you how to write posts"
Source URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alicjasmin_linkedin-is-literally-telling-you-how-to-activity-7282719529426636800-tlhy
(Based on 5,000+ LinkedIn posts of personal experience.)

Core advice:
- "Write the entire post in the first 3 lines" — LinkedIn truncates previews to ~3 lines (including blank/space lines) before "Read more."
- **Always write hooks as one-liners.** A hook spanning two lines = "a -20% drop" in engagement (his claim).
- Reason: a one-line hook leaves room for a SECOND sentence in the visible preview to add information that persuades the click. A two-line hook eats that real estate.
- "Optimize for the 'Read more' button. This is the most important button on LinkedIn. Treat those first 3 lines seriously."
- Tool: AuthoredUp to preview how the post truncates.

From getcreator.io profile (search result): Jasmin "leads with a curiosity-gap hook that delays the payoff, then delivers numbered lists that feel both scannable and substantive." Formula = hook strong enough to stop scroll + human angle + insights worth the time + simple CTA. ~3,662 engagements/post, has hit 1M+ impressions on a single post.
