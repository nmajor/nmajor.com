## The tension, stated plainly (this is the trade-off the report must resolve)
Two opposing schools, both partly right:

**Broadcast / broad relatability (reach):**
- "Easy: Creating a one-off viral post with a broad... topic. Difficult:
  consistently creating content on a specific topic your target audience is
  passionate about." (LinkedIn's own guide) — i.e., broad topics break out MORE
  EASILY; niche topics break out RARELY but reach the RIGHT people.
- Content-funnel model (Donnelly/Sanders): **Top-of-funnel = "go broad" to "drive
  mass attention"** (broad carousels, cheatsheets); **Middle-of-funnel = niche,
  story-driven** to build trust; **Bottom = convert.** Breadth and depth are
  different funnel JOBS, not competitors.

**Narrowcast / niche specificity (relevance, saves, conversion):**
- LinkedIn's own narrowcasting framework: "The broadcast approach says 'How can I
  reach the most people?' The narrowcast approach asks 'How can I reach exactly the
  right people?'" It claims narrowcasting yields SMALLER reach but "more relevant
  inbound" and "higher conversion from connection to conversation" — "all with
  SMALLER reach numbers."
- **The "this is for me" mechanism (verbatim):** "When someone feels content was
  created specifically for them, they're more likely to engage. Narrowcasting
  triggers the 'this is for me' response in your ideal audience." Tactics: name the
  audience in line 1, use industry terms only they'd know, ask questions only they
  can answer.
- Interest-graph matching REWARDS specificity for TARGETING: "The clearer your
  post's subject, the more distinct and matchable its embedding will be... This can
  help break you out of your network bubble and reach new people" (LinkedIn guide).
  So specificity helps the RIGHT strangers find you; breadth helps the MOST strangers
  find you.

## The resolution the report will use
These are two different WINS, not a contradiction:
- A **broadly-addressable, emotionally-relatable, news-pegged** post maximizes the
  size of the pool that can plausibly relate/react → BREAKOUT reach (Post A: every
  company has employees using AI shortcuts; every leader fears a compliance surprise).
- A **narrow, expert-specific** post (a governance checklist) maximizes RELEVANCE and
  SAVES within the ICP but its addressable pool is small, its emotional arousal is
  low, and resharing it signals little about the resharer → strong in-network
  engagement, no breakout.
The "addressable audience of a post" (how many people can see themselves in it) is a
distinct lever from "quality of fit." Breakout needs a big addressable pool AND
arousal AND a news peg; save-worthy niche content needs neither and shouldn't chase
them.

## Honest caveat (matches audience-strategy §2 and file 10)
LinkedIn's 2026 direction explicitly favors niche/topic CONSISTENCY for building
durable "topic authority" and warns against chasing broad clickbait. So the healthy
model is NOT "make everything broad." It is: a STEADY diet of niche, save-worthy,
ICP-specific posts that compound topic authority, PUNCTUATED by occasional
broadly-relatable, news-pegged breakout plays when a genuinely novel, first-of-kind
event lands in the lane. Breadth is the spike; niche depth is the baseline.
