## What they did
Netlify's four-person People team (Laura, SVP People; Kelly, HRBP Director; Jasmine,
People Operations Partner; and Chelsey Madsen, Head of Talent), none of them engineers,
started building small projects on Netlify to learn the platform and ended up replacing
several paid HR tools with internal apps they built themselves. They built a custom
employee survey app, a leadership hiring dashboard, a self-serve onboarding and
hiring-manager enablement tool, and a four-week psychological-safety learning series,
supporting a fully remote company of 100+ people across 12 countries.

## The numbers
- Before: paid employee-survey tool, separate hiring-analytics software, and an onboarding SaaS.
- After: in-house equivalents built on Netlify by non-engineers.
- Cost / spend: "saving over $10k in annual software costs" (self-reported by the team).
- Time / headcount / volume: "reclaiming hours each week"; the self-serve enablement tool
  replaced sessions where Chelsey spent "several hours at a time" onboarding hiring leaders
  one by one. No headcount change reported.

## What broke / the caveats
The $10k figure is self-reported in Netlify's own marketing blog, with no invoice-level
breakdown. Netlify is a developer-platform company and the team vibe-coded on its own
product, so this is close to a best case for non-engineers shipping internal tools. The
team is candid that the survey app pushed them past their depth on data security and
required pulling in engineering, security, and IT, which is the real cost the headline
saving omits.

## Why it matters for our beat
A non-technical HR team inside an established company replacing paid SaaS with tools they
built themselves is exactly the "ordinary team puts AI into a real workflow" story, and
the honest note about data-security complexity is the part most vendor case studies skip.
