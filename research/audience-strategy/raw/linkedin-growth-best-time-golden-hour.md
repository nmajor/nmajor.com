# Best time to post + the "golden hour" / first 90 minutes

Source: WebSearch "best time to post on LinkedIn 2026 first 90 minutes golden hour dwell time"
Accessed: 2026-06-19
Key sources: buffer.com/resources/best-time-to-post-on-linkedin (4.8M posts), expandi.io, supergrow.ai, postiv.ai, kanbox.io

## Verbatim claims

Best time: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-noon in audience's timezone (synthesis of 4 independent studies, 8M+ posts, 2B+ engagements). Buffer's separate study analyzed 4.8M posts.

Golden hour (first 60-90 min):
- LinkedIn shows your post to only 2-5% of your network in the first 60-90 minutes.
- "70% of a post's total reach is decided in this window."
- How that small group responds determines whether anyone else sees it.
- Posting at optimal times can increase impressions 30-50% vs off-peak.

Dwell time: LinkedIn tracks how long people pause on your post. Posting when people have time to actually read (morning coffee, lunch) increases dwell time, signaling quality.

Format-specific timing:
- Video best at 12-2pm and 5-7pm (lunch + post-work scroll)
- Polls live/die on first-hour momentum; Tue-Wed mid-morning optimal

Bottom line per sources: "Timing amplifies good content" — a multiplier, not a substitute for quality.

## REALITY CHECK / on-brand note
The "70% of reach decided in first 90 min" and "2-5% of network" are widely repeated marketing-blog numbers (folklore-adjacent; trace to van der Blom-style claims). Practical reality for a PART-TIME poster: timing matters as a multiplier but is NOT worth obsessing over. Post when YOUR audience (CTOs/founders, often US/EU business hours, Tue-Thu mornings) is online, then spend the first 30-60 min replying to every comment. That early reply activity is the controllable lever, not the exact minute of posting.
