# LinkedIn Algorithm 2026 + Link-in-Comments Debate — RAW
Access date: 2026-06-19

## Source: Melanie Goodman — "LinkedIn Algorithm 2026" (citing van der Blom + Saywhat)
URL: https://melaniegoodmanlinkedinconsultant.substack.com/p/linkedin-algorithm-2026-reach-topic-authority

Reach & distribution:
- Relationship Graph → Interest Graph shift. Only ~31% of feed from first-degree connections; ~25% from second/third-degree; ~10% algorithm-suggested.
- "Follower count and reach are structurally decoupled in the 2026 data."
- Average reach "permanently reset to a lower baseline" vs 2024. Platform engagement up ~18% vs six months prior.

External links (CONFLICTING DATA — important):
- **Van der Blom research (1.3M posts):** One external link in post body reduces median reach by **18.8%**. Comments with links suppressed by up to **80%**.
- **Saywhat analysis (~400K posts):** Posts with multiple external links performed "considerably better"; content quality appears to override link penalties.
- Takeaway: no-link posts get highest reach; the comments-as-workaround guidance is now contested/dated. The 2026 algorithm reportedly detects "bridge behavior" (post designed to funnel to a link in comments) and applies similar penalties. [TREAT AS DIRECTIONAL, not absolute — sources conflict.]

Posting cadence (from this source):
- Optimal: **2–4 posts weekly.**
- Moving 1 → 2-4 posts adds ~1,234 impressions per post.
- Daily posting causes **26% drop in average reach per post.**
- Long breaks without topic re-anchoring → deprioritization.

Hashtags:
- Posts WITHOUT hashtags outperform those with by **5–10%.**
- Recommended 0–3 (max 5 for events). 10+ risks **30–50% visibility penalty.**

Topic Authority: built from topic consistency + engagement quality + semantic clarity. (Implication for an anti-hype AI brand: stay on-topic to compound authority.)

Sources cited: Richard van der Blom, LinkedIn Algorithm Report 2026; Saywhat Q1 2026 analysis.

- "One external link in the post body reduces median reach by 18.8%" (van der Blom 2026).
- Some sources state external links cut reach "by roughly 60%" [HIGHER/UNVERIFIED — likely older/inflated; van der Blom's 18.8% is the more credible figure].
- "LinkedIn now suppresses comments containing external links, with visibility reduced by up to 80%." → The classic "link in first comment" trick is weakened.
- Conflicting Q1 2026 analysis: posts with multiple external links performed better (quality override).

## FOLKLORE FLAGS
- "External links cut reach by 60%" — widely repeated, likely inflated/older. The 18.8% (van der Blom, 1.3M posts) is better-sourced.
- "Always put your link in the first comment" — was true ~2021-2023, now CONTESTED for 2026. Test both; don't treat as gospel.
- Carousel "24.42% engagement rate" stat (see carousel file) — engagement RATE is not reach; high number partly reflects format math (multi-slide dwell), not magic.
