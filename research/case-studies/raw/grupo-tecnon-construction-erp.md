# €20M construction company built its own ERP, zero developers

## Person / company
- Miguel, age 35, Spain (Almería). Background in law and politics, not engineering.
- CEO of Grupo Tecnon, a construction firm.
- €20M annual revenue, 50 direct employees, ~300 contractors, 0 developers hired.

## What he built
A unified system from a single React Native (Expo) codebase across three surfaces:
- Web app (office command center).
- iOS + Android apps (field technician companions).
Functions: client lifecycle/CRM (lead -> construction), field service mobile app with
voice-to-report (technician records observations; AI transcribes into structured
reports), auto-generated contracts with e-signature, real-time ops dashboard (team
activity, cash flow, payables, invoicing).

## What it replaced
Spreadsheets, budgeting software, email workflows, multiple SaaS subscriptions. He
evaluated Procore, Buildertrend, JobTread and Spanish ERPs; found them too expensive or
misaligned with a subcontractor-heavy workflow.

## Numbers (all vendor-reported)
- Eliminated multiple SaaS license fees (no per-tool figure given).
- "Avoided $50,000–$150,000+ custom development costs plus 15–25% annual maintenance."
- No developer salaries / no bus-factor risk.
- Contract turnaround "from days to one click."
Quote: "I have ideas in my head I could never build. Now I can."

## Platform
Built on Rork (AI app builder), ChatGPT for feature planning, Supabase for the database.
Miguel owns the code, DB and app accounts (Rork's framing: no vendor lock-in).
