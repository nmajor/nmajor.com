# The generative-vs-predictive distinction (industry framing) + 2026 prior-auth ROLLBACK context
Sources fetched 2026-07-07.

## Health Affairs — "The AI Arms Race In Health Insurance Utilization Review" (2026)
https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2025.00897
Verbatim (defines the exact distinction we must preserve):
"Offerings include both generative and predictive AI tools. Generative AI tools use large language models (similar to ChatGPT) to create novel text in response to a prompt entered by the user. Predictive AI tools use machine learning techniques to forecast an outcome (for example, days of care required or the likelihood that a denial would be overturned if appealed) or classify something into a category..."
Glossary: "'Prior authorization' = provider's request for approval before a service. 'Concurrent review' = insurer review after care begins. 'Claims adjudication' = insurer review of a claim after service rendered. 'Postpayment audit' = insurer search for fraud/errors."
=> nH Predict is squarely the "predictive/ML" type (forecasts days of care / appeal likelihood). Generative/LLM is a DIFFERENT category.
