## What they did
Allianz Partners, the assistance and insurance arm handling health, automotive, and travel claims,
is moving from a people-scaled claims operation to one built on autonomous AI agents from vendor
Otera. Per the vendor, the old model scaled linearly with volume (more cases meant more staff, with
no margin leverage), and Allianz is replacing it, with a global rollout described as already live
across multiple markets.

## The numbers
- 90M+ cases processed per year across health, auto, and travel (vendor-reported).
- A single claim could take up to 29 days to resolve end-to-end across dozens of disconnected
  systems (vendor-reported baseline).
- After-state metrics (resolution time, cost, headcount, automation rate): not given.

## What broke / the caveats
This is an Otera (vendor) blog post and the weakest kind of evidence: it states the scale and the
problem vividly but gives no measured outcome. There is no after-number for resolution time,
automation rate, cost, or accuracy, only the claim that the old model is being "replaced" and a
rollout is "live." The named company and the 90M-cases / 29-days baseline are the usable signal; the
"replacing claims operations with autonomous agents" framing is marketing until corroborated. Needs
independent confirmation before any post leans on it.

## Why it matters for our beat
A top-tier insurer's assistance business at genuinely large scale (90M cases) is a marquee
claims-automation target, and claims is one of our core insurance workflows. Worth tracking even
though the current evidence is thin, because the named deployment is real and big.
