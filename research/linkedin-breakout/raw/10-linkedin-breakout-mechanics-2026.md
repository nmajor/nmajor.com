## The distribution cascade (widely corroborated direction; exact % = FOLKLORE)
1. **Initial sample.** LinkedIn first shows a post to a small % of your network —
   commonly cited as **~2-5%** (LinkedGrow) or **~6-8% of followers** (Collins).
   [FOLKLORE FLAG: exact % is repeated across blogs, not from LinkedIn engineering;
   matches the "2-5% / golden hour" folklore already flagged in
   audience-strategy/report.md §2.]
2. **The test window ("golden hour," ~first 60-90 min).** The algorithm watches how
   that sample engages. Strong early engagement → the post is pushed outward.
   [The exact "70% of reach decided in 90 min" figure remains FOLKLORE — see
   audience-strategy §2. Direction — early engagement gates expansion — is
   consistently reported by Hootsuite and LinkedIn's own comms.]
3. **Expansion to 2nd/3rd-degree + the interest graph.** "Posts that get strong
   interaction in the first hour will now be shown to second- and third-degree
   connections more frequently" (Hootsuite). Expansion "continues for 48-72 hours if
   engagement remains strong," and strong posts "resurface in feeds for two to three
   weeks" (LinkedGrow) — a structural advantage over X/Instagram's ~24h shelf life.

## What specifically triggers ESCAPE from the network (the breakout levers)
- **The shift from Relationship Graph to Interest Graph.** (Corroborates
  audience-strategy §2.) "Your content is no longer shown mainly to people who know
  you. It is shown to people interested in the topic" (Collins). The escape hatch is
  TOPICAL/INTEREST match, not who you know.
- **A 2nd/3rd-degree comment is ~3x more valuable than a 1st-degree comment**
  (Collins, rule 5). Engagement from STRANGERS is the signal that tells LinkedIn "this
  travels beyond his bubble" — the literal breakout signal. [FOLKLORE magnitude;
  direction repeated widely.]
- **The 2026 engagement hierarchy (practitioner consensus, Maja Voje):** "comments
  matter most, then saves, then shares, then reposts, then likes." Note the tension
  this creates with Twitter/Facebook virality research (where the RESHARE is the
  breakout multiplier): on LinkedIn's interest-graph feed in 2026, a stranger's
  COMMENT is weighted at least as heavily as a reshare for expanding distribution.
  [FOLKLORE: the exact ordering varies by source — Collins lists saves as "the new
  currency"; treat as directional. What's solid: likes are the weakest signal;
  comments/saves/shares from beyond your network are what expand reach.]
- **Dwell time.** More weight on how long people read (Hootsuite). Longer-read posts
  get better distribution.

## The 2026 "anti-virality" turn — the crucial reconciliation
Multiple 2026 sources claim LinkedIn deliberately DE-prioritized viral tactics:
- LinkedGrow (paraphrasing LinkedIn): "LinkedIn has made a deliberate shift away from
  viral content. The platform publicly stated that 60% of high-engagement posts in
  2025 used tactics that did not actually drive user satisfaction [engagement bait,
  rage posts, generic motivational quotes]. The algorithm now deprioritizes these and
  rewards genuine professional value." [The "60%" figure is FOLKLORE — echoes the
  "60% engagement-bait penalty" already flagged in content-hooks/report.md §1. The
  DIRECTION (bait down, depth up) is well-supported and matches LinkedIn's own comms.]
- 360Brew: LinkedIn's 2026 foundation model reportedly "evaluates people and
  companies as a body of work, not just individual posts" and detects AI-patterned/
  templated writing (Kyle Gawley thread). [Vendor description; treat as directional.]

**Reconciliation (important for the report):** "LinkedIn killed virality" and "Post A
hit 171k impressions" are BOTH true and not contradictory. What LinkedIn suppressed
is CHEAP virality — engagement-bait, rage, empty motivation. What still breaks out is
a genuinely valuable, high-interest, broadly-relevant, emotionally-activating,
topical post that strangers engage with substantively. Post A is the LEGITIMATE
breakout the 2026 algorithm still rewards: real news, real stakes, real question,
substantive stranger comments — not a bait trick. So the anti-hype path and the
breakout path are NOT opposed; the platform now specifically rewards the honest
version and punishes the manipulative one.
