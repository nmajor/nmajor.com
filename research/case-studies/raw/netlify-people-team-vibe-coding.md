First-person account by Chelsey (Head of Talent) on the Netlify blog.

The team: four-person People department at Netlify supporting a fully remote org of
100+ employees across 12 countries.
- Laura (SVP, People)
- Kelly (HRBP Director)
- Jasmine (People Operations Partner)
- Chelsey Madsen (Head of Talent)

None are engineers. Started by building "websites for their pets" as a low-stakes way
to learn the platform, then moved to production internal tools.

SaaS replaced -> built in-house (vibe coded on Netlify):
- Employee survey platform -> custom internal pulse/sentiment survey app with admin analytics backend
- Hiring analytics tool -> tailored leadership hiring dashboard (role breakdowns, pipeline metrics, YTD data)
- Onboarding SaaS -> self-serve onboarding experience / gamified training
- Generic hiring enablement -> Hiring Manager Enablement App (interactive, embedded videos and quizzes)
- Also built: Psychological Safety Learning Series (4-week self-serve program)

Numbers (self-reported by the team in their own blog post):
- "saving over $10k in annual software costs"
- "reclaiming hours each week"
- Before the self-serve enablement tool, Chelsey spent "several hours at a time enabling
  new hiring leaders individually."

Caveats:
- Kelly on the survey app: "The complexity of this project from a technical depth and data
  security perspective forced me out of my comfort zone in a good way" - required
  collaboration with engineering, security, and IT rather than staying isolated HR work.
- Netlify is a developer-platform company (vibe coding on its own product), so this is a
  best case for non-engineers building internal tools; the data-security learning curve is
  named explicitly.
