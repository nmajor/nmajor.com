"They barely mention AI, so they must be behind." In banking, that read is backwards.

A census from the Institute of Applied AI put banks and lenders dead last for mentioning AI, at 22.8%, below every other traditional sector. The tempting conclusion is that banks are slow. The opposite is closer to true.

The same banks are running AI hard. JPMorgan alone has hundreds of generative-AI projects and put 200,000 staff on an internal assistant in months. The filing measures willingness to say so on the record, not how much is deployed. Point a model at a lending decision and every public claim about it becomes a fair-lending or securities-disclosure surface, so the heaviest users go quiet.

So stop reading a company's AI maturity off its filings and press releases. That number tracks legal exposure and disclosure rules as much as real capability. If you actually want to gauge a competitor, ignore what they announce and read what leaks:

- who they are hiring, and whether the job posts describe real systems
- where their compute and vendor spend is going
- what their own customers and case studies quietly reveal

Those are harder to dress up than a filing.

The next time a rival seems quiet on AI, don't assume they are asleep. Assume they may have more to lose by talking than you do.
