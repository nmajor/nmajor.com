# 0 to 100k guide (creatoreconomy.so) + consistency vs volume

Accessed: 2026-06-19

## creatoreconomy.so 7-step guide (author hit 100k+ in one year)
Source: https://creatoreconomy.so/p/complete-guide-to-growing-on-linkedin
1. Profile setup: enable creator mode (allow followers); banner highlighting target user's desired OUTCOME; hashtags + featured section; compelling About explaining why to follow.
2. Engage with top creators: follow niche creators, find their best posts, reply with valuable insights, avoid self-promo.
3. Post consistently: daily (uses scheduling); batch a week ahead; wait 2-4 weeks before analyzing.
4. Master opening lines: "the first 2 lines of your LinkedIn post need to pique interest enough to click 'See More'." Each line under 45 chars. Line 1 = bold attention-grab; Line 2 = hook compelling expand.
5. Analyze after 2-4 weeks; find patterns in impressions/interactions; do more of what resonates while staying authentic.
6. Strategic link placement: links at END of longer posts with image/video to mitigate downranking.
7. Contribute to collaborative articles to unlock niche "Top Voice" badge.
Example high-performing hooks: "Shopify deleted 12,000 meetings this year"; "Amazon has an incredible writing culture" (5K+ likes).

## Consistency vs volume (the most important sustainability finding)
Sources: buffer.com/resources/linkedin-consistency, medium Michael Taylor, ritnerdigital, shieldapp
- "Consistency beats intensity — better to post 8x/month for 24 months than 20x/month for 6 months then quit." "Better to post twice a week every week for a year than daily for a month then quit."
- Sustainability is priority #1: "the fastest way to stall growth is to burn through your best ideas in a short burst and then disappear."
- High-frequency works only WITH content support (ghostwriter/editor); for solo operators it's rarely sustainable.
- For most personal brands: 3-5 posts/week — frequent enough to stay in algorithm's "creator" category while giving time to produce content worth reading.
- COMPOUNDING: content library builds credibility; "creator" classification deepens; "Most professionals who stick with it start seeing meaningful inbound activity around the 3-6 month mark."
- "Quality beats frequency."

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID
- ON-BRAND (critical for a PART-TIME anti-hype expert): pick a sustainable cadence (2-4/week) you can hold for a YEAR; expect inbound at 3-6 months; consistency > bursts; quality/saves over volume.
- AVOID/CAUTION: "post daily" advice assumes content support; LinkedIn Premium just for a "Visit my website" link is optional/low-priority; manufactured/curiosity-bait hooks ("X deleted 12,000 meetings") can drift clickbait — anti-hype version is a true, specific, surprising fact stated plainly, not a manufactured cliffhanger.
