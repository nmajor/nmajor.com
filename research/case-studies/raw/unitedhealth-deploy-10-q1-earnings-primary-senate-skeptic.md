## Skeptic's read — the "savings may be avoided payouts, not efficiency" argument
Best-sourced version = the Lokken plaintiffs' theory itself (see file 09): a denial-driven "financial windfall in the form of policy premiums without having to pay for promised care." This is well-sourced but pertains to nH Predict (older ML tool), NOT the 2026 generative push.
Financial-analysis version (WEAKER source — ainvest.com, flagged low-trust): a 2:1 return on $1.5B implies ~$3B annual savings; against UNH 2025 operating income ~$19B that would be ~15.8% of operating profit from automation in one year — presented as implausibly large, raising the question of whether "savings" are efficiency vs deferred/denied liability. Treat the specific math as SPECULATIVE and the source as low-trust; the underlying question is legitimate but no primary source quantifies "denial-driven" savings.
