# Content and SEO governance across the three properties

Date: 2026-06-30. This is the canonical governance doc for how content and SEO are divided
across the Institute, the Association, and the consultancy, so the Institute never
cannibalizes the consultancy's commercial SEO. It is a rulebook plus an intent-assignment
table. Change a rule here.

Grounded in: `overview.md` ("Independence and firewall", the sister-org section, the
Institute's shift to an original-data / research publisher), `research/keyword-research/report.md`
(the real clusters, intent labels, and CPCs), `research/disclosure-conventions/report.md`
(what the Institute may and may not say about the consultancy), and
`research/audience-strategy/report.md` (the funnel, now re-homed off the Institute; its
personal-brand-as-reach-engine finding still holds). House style is the `writing-voice` skill.
The page-by-page build lives in `research/governance-content-map/strategy.md`; this doc sets
the rules that map obeys.

## North star

The consultancy is the business. Everything else exists to grow it. The Institute and the
Association are strictly non-commercial in conduct: they sell nothing, solicit nothing, and
run no funnel (`overview.md`, "Independence and firewall"). So content and SEO are arranged to
build the consultancy's authority and audience without the orgs ever selling. The Institute's
job is credibility, original data and topical authority, an owned email list, and a disclosed
bridge that passes authority to the consultancy. The selling happens only on the consultancy's
own turf, and only after a prospect crosses the commercial line on their own.

## The hard rule

**One canonical owner per keyword and per search intent, across all properties.** A keyword or
intent belongs to exactly one of our domains. No two of our own sites target the same term.

This is not tidiness, it is self-defense. If the Institute ranks for a buyer term, two bad
things happen at once. Its higher-authority, non-selling page outranks the consultancy's page
and starves it of the click (self-cannibalization), and the click it does win cannot convert,
because the firewall forbids a CTA. So the Institute deliberately does not target commercial
terms at all. When a topic has both an informational and a commercial version, treat them as
two different keywords with two different owners (see the intent table).

## The rules

1. **One canonical owner per keyword and intent** (above). The firewall in `overview.md`
   stays intact. The Institute has no "money pages"; the paid "Programs" were removed.
2. **The Institute owns** informational, educational, and definitional terms; original
   data, statistics, and benchmarks (the highest link-earning, most AI-citable content);
   neutral comparisons; industry use-case explainers written as education; and named-expert
   Conversations. It **deliberately does not target** commercial, buyer, or transactional terms.
3. **The consultancy owns** all commercial and buyer intent: "services", "consultant",
   "agency", "implementation", "audit", "automation company", "for [industry]" buyer framing,
   pricing, "how to choose / hire", and tool / solution / platform buyer guides. It also owns
   case studies and opinionated POV. Mid-funnel "evaluating a solution or a partner" is
   commercial, so it goes to the consultancy.
4. **The buyer-ready test** decides any term (see the next section). High commercial CPC, or a
   query containing services / cost / price / hire / "best [tool]" / vendor / agency /
   consultant, goes to the consultancy. "What is / how / challenges / examples / neutral
   framework / maturity model / use cases" goes to the Institute.
5. **Cross-links go one way and stay factual.** The Institute links to the consultancy only as
   disclosure (the conflict-of-interest page, founder bios), never as a keyword-anchored funnel
   CTA. These disclosure links legitimately pass authority and brand to the new consultancy
   domain. That is the white-hat way Institute authority helps the consultancy.
6. **Newsletter and voice split.** The Institute newsletter is a research cadence: a new
   report, benchmark, or Conversation, in a neutral institutional voice, not one founder's
   personal takes. Nick's personal writing and POV live on his personal channel. The commercial
   nurture list belongs to the consultancy (or Nick), not the Institute.
7. **Near-term reality.** SEO is a 6-18 month game and the consultancy domain starts at zero.
   Near-term consultancy growth comes from Nick's personal brand and reputation pull, not SEO.
8. **North star** (above): grow the consultancy. The Institute's role is credibility, data and
   topical authority, the email list, and a disclosed authority-passing bridge, not selling.

## The buyer-ready test

A fast rule an operator can apply to any keyword before assigning it.

| Signal in the query | Likely owner |
|---|---|
| `services`, `cost`, `price`, `hire`, `best [tool]`, `vendor`, `agency`, `consultant`, `software`, `for [industry]` (buyer framing) | Consultancy |
| `what is`, `how`, `challenges`, `examples`, `use cases`, `framework`, `maturity model`, `roles`, `committee`, `template`, `benchmark` | Institute |
| High commercial CPC (real ad budget behind it) | Consultancy |
| Blank or low CPC plus an explainer modifier | Institute |

**Tiebreaker when the modifier and the CPC disagree.** CPC is the money signal; the modifier
is the framing signal. Read both, not either alone. A buyer modifier plus a high CPC goes to
the consultancy. An explainer modifier plus a low or absent CPC goes to the Institute. When
still unsure, eyeball the live SERP (`research/keyword-research/report.md`, section 7): if
vendor and pricing pages own it, it is commercial; if explainers and Wikipedia own it, it is
informational.

One caution on the data: the "intent" column in keyword-research is derived from advertiser
competition, so some rows read `commercial` while having little or no buyer budget. Use the
test above, not that label, as the deciding signal.

### Worked examples of the test

- **`ai governance` (5,400/mo, CPC $45.87).** The CPC is high, which by itself points at the
  consultancy. But the modifier is the bare definitional head, with no buyer verb; the budget
  reflects advertisers bidding on a broad term, not a buyer's decision query. Modifier wins.
  **Institute** (it is the definitional pillar).
- **`ai governance solutions` (2,400/mo, CPC $144.52).** "Solutions" is buyer framing and the
  CPC is the highest in the cluster. Modifier and money agree. **Consultancy.**
- **`ai readiness assessment` vs `ai readiness assessment services`.** Same topic, the intent
  splits the keyword. "Assessment" (720/mo, CPC $34.63) is informational and feeds the free
  benchmark: **Institute.** "...services" (70/mo, CPC $104.28) is a buyer shopping for the paid
  audit: **Consultancy.** Same demand, two owners, split by intent.
- **`ai for accounting` (6,600/mo, CPC $23.35).** "For [industry]" is buyer framing.
  **Consultancy.** The Institute's neutral counterpart is `ai use cases in finance` (90/mo),
  an educational roundup, which stays **Institute**. Different keywords, no overlap.
- **`ai governance challenges` (1,000/mo, diff 9, no CPC).** Labeled `commercial` in the data,
  but "challenges" is an explainer modifier and there is no ad budget. The label is not the
  buyer signal. **Institute.**
- **`agentic ai governance framework` (50/mo, CPC $63.70).** A genuine conflict: "framework"
  says Institute, $63.70 CPC says consultancy. Tiebreaker resolves to **Institute** (it is a
  framework explainer on a fast-rising term, +267%). The consultancy may later build a buyer
  page on a different keyword without reusing this one.

## Cross-links between properties

The firewall caps Institute-to-consultancy links at the factual disclosures it already permits
(`overview.md`, "Independence and firewall"; `research/disclosure-conventions/report.md`):

- The Institute's `/independence` (conflict-of-interest / editorial-independence) page.
- Founder bios that name the consultancy as a plain biographical fact.

These pass authority and brand to the new consultancy domain, which starts at zero, without
being a funnel. Anchor-text rules:

- Keep it factual. Use the consultancy's name or a plain phrase, never a keyword-stuffed
  anchor. Allowed: "Nick also runs [consultancy name]." Not allowed: "best AI automation consultant".
- No "hire us / book a call / work with us" CTAs anywhere on the Institute or Association,
  including the assessment results, newsletter, posts, and roundtables.
- No paid retargeting of Institute or Association visitors or the list with consultancy ads.
- At most one quiet disclosure line under the AI Readiness Assessment results, framed as
  disclosure, not invitation.

## Reality check

- SEO is a 6-18 month compounding game and the consultancy domain starts at zero. Even diff-10
  terms take time and links (`research/keyword-research/report.md`, section 7).
- Near-term consultancy growth comes from Nick's personal brand and reputation pull, not SEO.
  Personal profiles out-reach company pages 5-8x, and meaningful inbound is expected around the
  3-6 month mark from consistent personal posting (`research/audience-strategy/report.md`).
- The Institute's SEO role is authority, link-earning original data, the owned list, and the
  disclosed bridge. It earns the right to be found and trusted; the consultancy sells.

## One-line summary

One owner per keyword and intent: the Institute owns informational and original-data terms and
never sells, the consultancy owns every commercial term and is the only place selling happens,
the Association has no SEO play, and Nick's personal brand is the near-term reach engine that
feeds the consultancy by pull.
