# Raw findings: Anatomy of a high-performing B2B LinkedIn TEXT post (2025–2026)

Topic: structure + formatting craft that drives dwell time, saves, and meaningful
comments for a credibility-first, anti-hype expert audience. Focus on TEXT posts
(not carousels/video), since that is our top-of-funnel format.

Collected 2026-06-29. Sources cited inline. FOLKLORE / unverified vendor claims are
flagged. Distinguish: (a) primary data studies (van der Blom 1.8M posts; AuthoredUp
372k–847k posts; Edelman-LinkedIn ~2,000 buyers) vs (b) vendor blog folklore that
recycles round numbers without method.

---

## 0. CONTEXT THAT REFRAMES EVERYTHING: text posts are the WEAK format on reach

Multiple sources converge: plain text posts are at or below baseline reach multiplier,
and reach overall has fallen hard. This matters for our strategy — we want text posts
for credibility, so we have to win on the *quality* signals (dwell, saves, real
comments), not on format-gaming.

- Richard van der Blom, **Algorithm Insights Report 2025** (analysis of **1.8M+
  posts**): reach format multipliers on personal profiles —
  - Polls 1.64x, Documents (PDF carousels) 1.45x, Images 1.18x, Video 1.10x,
    **Text posts 0.88x** (i.e. text is *below* baseline).
  - Source (secondary summary): https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
  - Report landing: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_chapter-1-algorithm-insights-report-2025-activity-7322514599126130688-Q895
  - Chapter 1 PDF mirror: https://www.scribd.com/document/984921783/Algorithm-Insights-Report-2025-chapter-1-Richard-Van-der-Blom
- Same report: **Views down ~50%, engagement down ~25%, follower growth down ~59%**
  YoY. Average post reach fell to **8–12% of followers** (was 15–20% a year earlier).
  - Document/PDF carousels hit **6.60% engagement**, highest of any format; **standard
    text posts struggle to break 2%**.
  - Source: https://www.dataslayer.ai/blog/linkedin-algorithm-february-2026-whats-working-now
    and https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-algorithm-explained
- AuthoredUp's own 372,126-post dataset (Sept 2025–Feb 2026) shows text-post median
  engagement clustering ~2.1–2.7% depending on length (below). So "win" for a serious
  text post = ~2.6%+ ER, plus saves/dwell, not viral numbers.

> **Implication for us:** We are choosing the lowest-reach format on purpose, for
> credibility. So every text post must over-index on the things text CAN win:
> dwell time, saves, and 15+ word comments. Occasionally pairing a text post with a
> single relevant image (1.18x) or turning a flagship piece into a document/carousel
> is the cheap lever if we ever want more raw reach. (van der Blom, above.)

## 1. POST STRUCTURE — the anatomy

### 1a. The fold ("see more" truncation) — the hard constraint everything bends around

- **~210 characters on desktop, ~140 on mobile** appear before the "...see more"
  prompt. Hard post cap is **3,000 characters** (spaces, line breaks, emojis count).
  - Source (AuthoredUp, primary-ish reference): https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-character-limit
- Mobile fold varies by device: ~**140 chars** on a small phone portrait, ~180 on a
  larger phone, 210+ on tablet. **57% of LinkedIn traffic is mobile**, so write the
  hook to land inside the **mobile ~140-char cutoff** so it works on both.
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-character-limit (search summary)
- Better mental model than "210 chars": **think in lines, not characters.** "Line 1 +
  line 2" is the visible unit. If line 1 is a sentence (~70 chars) and line 2 is a
  blank line break, the reader sees a clean hook. If line 1 is a 200-char run-on,
  mobile users see a wall and scroll. (AuthoredUp summary, same source.)
- Practical rule restated across sources: the first **2–3 lines do ALL the work**; if
  they don't stop the scroll, the other ~2,800 characters are invisible.

### 1b. Named body structures (with what each is for)

From the GrackerAI "Anatomy of a Viral B2B Post" breakdown
(https://gracker.ai/blog/viral-linkedin-b2b-post-anatomy) — a 7-part skeleton. Note:
this is a marketing-blog framework (treat the *structure* as sound, the exact
character buckets as **vendor folklore**, not measured):

1. **Hook** (first ~49 chars / line 1): bold statement, unexpected stat, named
   failure, or industry tension — before the fold.
2. **Re-hook** (next ~100–150 chars, still above/near fold): reinforce + add a
   concrete specific so the "see more" click is earned.
3. **(Optional) visual**: image/carousel. (Out of scope for pure text, but note it
   exists.)
4. **Body** (~400–650 chars in their model): short sentences (<20 words),
   single-line or small paragraphs, one idea each, specific examples + data.
5. **Close** (~130 chars): land the point, set up the ask.
6. **Primary CTA**: ONE specific action.
7. **Secondary CTA**: soft ("follow for more X").
   - FLAG: their worked example ("How I track SEO leads," 172 likes/123 comments)
     relies on an **infographic**, so it isn't a pure-text proof point. Character
     buckets are illustrative, not from a study.

**Named writing frameworks people actually use for the body** (folklore-grade as to
which "wins," but they are real, recognized structures):

- **PAS — Problem / Agitate / Solve.** Open with the reader's problem, sharpen the
  cost of it, then resolve. Good for "here's a mistake operators make" posts.
- **Story arc** — setup → tension/turn → resolution → lesson. The strongest *hook*
  type per several vendors (see §3), but the highest broetry risk (see §2).
- **Listicle / numbered framework** — "5 things," numbered steps. Highest *save* rate
  format because it's reusable/reference-able (see §3, dewey + stackmatix).
- **Framework / mental model** — name a model, define it, show it applied. Strong
  for saves + authority.
- **Contrarian / myth-bust** — "Everyone says X. The data says Y." Edelman backs this:
  86% of buyers prefer content that *challenges their assumptions* (§5).

### 1c. The close / CTA — earn the comment, don't beg it

- The honest close is a **genuine open question tied to the reader's experience**, or
  a clear "here's what I'd do," NOT "comment YES." Engagement-bait CTAs ("comment YES
  if you agree," reaction-polling) are now **detected and penalized** (see §3).
- Vendor guidance: one *specific* primary CTA beats a generic "thoughts?" — but for an
  anti-hype brand the safest high-quality CTA is a real question that only someone
  with relevant experience can answer (drives the 15+ word comments the algorithm
  rewards). Source: https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/linkedin-content-strategy-b2b

## 2. FORMATTING MECHANICS

### 2a. What LinkedIn natively supports

- ONLY: **line breaks / white space, emojis, plain text** (letters, numbers,
  punctuation, hashtags, mentions). **No native bold, italics, underline, headings.**
  - Source: https://magicpost.in/blog/linkedin-post-formatting
- "Unicode bold/italics" (the 𝗳𝗮𝗸𝗲-bold trick): NOT native. **Accessibility problem —
  screen readers read Unicode 'bold' as gibberish**, renders inconsistently, and looks
  automated if overused. For a credibility-first brand, **avoid it.** (magicpost, same.)

### 2b. White space & line length — the line that separates "scannable" from "broetry"

- Line breaks/white space = "the single most powerful formatting tool" on LinkedIn —
  because it controls how the hook renders at the fold and keeps the body scannable.
  (magicpost.in, above.)
- The CRAFT line: short paragraphs and white space = good (scannable, mobile-friendly).
  **One-sentence-per-line for the WHOLE post = broetry** = now a *liability*.
- **Broetry defined:** single-sentence paragraphs, dramatic line breaks, sensationalized
  personal anecdotes, "pseudo-profundity," soundbites over substance. Term coined by
  Bloomberg's Lorcan Roche Kelly.
  - Sources: https://medium.com/@txsling/txsl-3-cool-linkedin-post-bro-why-broetry-sucks-d548ac945aa1
    ; https://contentmarketinginstitute.com/articles/avoid-broetry-writing-trend/
    ; https://fenwick.media/rewild/magazine/dead-broets-society-behind-the-strange-story
- WHY broetry happened: it gamed dwell time / "see more" clicks. The algorithm read
  the click + scroll-through as engagement. (CMI + fenwick, above.)
- WHY it's now a liability (2025–2026): LinkedIn "waged war on engagement bait"; the
  algorithm now **penalizes low-value formatting** and rewards **dwell time, knowledge
  value, and meaningful comments**. "LinkedIn's core objective in 2026 is to be a
  professional learning platform, not a diary."
  - Source: https://www.sophieflow.com/article/the-end-of-linkedin-broetry-why-value-driven-ai-storytelling-is-winning-in-2026
  - FLAG: "the algorithm penalizes broetry formatting" specifically is **asserted by
    vendors, not proven by a published study.** What IS documented: engagement-bait
    CTAs are penalized, and reach for shallow content has collapsed. The safe read:
    broetry no longer *helps* and signals low-credibility to our exact audience.
- **Heuristic for us (synthesized, label as heuristic):** vary paragraph length —
  mostly 1–3 short lines per paragraph, a few single lines for *emphasis only*, never
  a whole post of orphaned single lines. White space to aid reading, not to manufacture
  drama.

### 2c. Ideal total length for B2B thought leadership

- **AuthoredUp, 372,126 posts (Sept 2025–Feb 2026):** the **1,301–2,500 character**
  band is the sweet spot.
  - 1,301–2,000 chars → **2.61%** median ER
  - 2,001–2,500 chars → **2.67%** median ER
  - under 400 chars → **2.10%** ER
  - So mid-length posts get **~27% higher engagement than sub-400-char posts.**
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-character-limit
- van der Blom: optimal **800–1,000 chars** (personal), 600–800 (company page); but the
  **top 5% of posts average 1,700+ characters / 16–20 sentences.**
  - Source (secondary): https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
- **Reconciliation:** the data supports **substantial, mid-to-long text (roughly
  ~1,300–2,000 chars / ~250–350 words)** for thought leadership — NOT one-liners and
  NOT 3,000-char essays. This is GOOD news for an anti-hype brand: depth is rewarded.

### 2d. Readability — write for grade 5–7, not grade 11

- Posts with reading level **above 10th grade get ~35% less reach**; the recurring
  guidance is to target roughly a **4th–7th grade reading level.**
  - Source (secondary, van der Blom-derived): https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
  - FLAG: the exact "−35% above 10th grade" figure traces to vendor summaries of van
    der Blom, not an independently published table — treat as directional, not exact.
    The *direction* (simple, plain language wins) is consistent everywhere.
- For us this aligns perfectly with the writing-voice skill: short words, concrete
  nouns, no jargon. Depth of *idea* at a low *reading grade* — dense, not complicated.

### 2e. Emojis — one, as structure, not decoration

- **AuthoredUp, 847,000+ posts:** going from **0 → 1 emoji** lifts ER 2.30% → 2.47%
  (**+7% ER**) and reach 798 → 970 (**+22% reach**). ER is **flat from 1 to 15
  emojis** (2.46–2.56%) — more emojis neither help nor hurt ER much.
  - Guidance: use emojis **"as structure, not decoration"** (mark sections, replace
    bullets, flag a key number); don't stack them at sentence ends.
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/how-to-add-emojis-on-linkedin-posts
  - FLAG: a +22% reach swing from one emoji is correlation in observational data, not
    causal — people who use 1 purposeful emoji may simply write better posts. Safe
    read: **a sparing, structural emoji is fine and slightly helps; emoji spam signals
    automation.** For an anti-hype voice, near-zero or 1 functional marker.
- Bullets: LinkedIn has **no native bullet list**, so people fake them with emoji/
  characters (•, →, ✅). Use sparingly and consistently. (magicpost.in.)

### 2f. Hashtags — few or none

- LinkedIn **disabled hashtag follow pages in October 2024**; hashtags lost most
  discovery value. Using **more than ~6 hashtags can hurt reach**; "5–10 tags" advice
  is dead.
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm (and magicpost.in)
- Practical: **0–3 relevant hashtags** max, at the end. For a credibility brand, 1–3
  tightly-relevant tags or none.

### 2g. Links — the external-link penalty

- van der Blom-era data: single-link posts perform worst; one claim that "4+ links →
  3–5x higher median reach" (FLAG: **counterintuitive, vendor-reported, weak** — likely
  an artifact, do not rely on it).
- The robust, repeated finding: **outbound links in the post body suppress reach**
  (LinkedIn wants users to stay). 2026 vendor claim: external links **penalized ~60%.**
  - Sources: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm ;
    https://getdewey.co/blog/linkedin-2026-algorithm-saves/
  - Practical convention (well-established folklore, widely practiced): **put the link
    in the first comment, or say "link in comments,"** rather than in the post body.
    FLAG: "first comment beats body link" is practitioner consensus, not a published
    controlled study, but it's the safe default.

## 3. ENGAGEMENT WITHOUT BAIT — dwell, saves, real comments

### 3a. Dwell time

- **Posts with 61+ seconds dwell time → 15.6% engagement; 0–3 seconds → 1.2%.** Dwell
  time is weighted heavily, above likes.
  - Source: https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-algorithm-explained
  - FLAG: the precise 15.6% vs 1.2% pairing recurs across vendor blogs citing van der
    Blom; treat magnitudes as directional. The *mechanism* (longer read = more reach)
    is consistent and is the whole reason long-but-readable substance wins.
- How to earn dwell honestly: a real hook + a body worth reading to the end (story arc,
  a framework that pays off, data the reader didn't have). NOT artificial "see more"
  cliffhangers (that's the broetry trick that's now devalued).

### 3b. Saves — the metric to optimize for an expert audience

- **Saves are the strongest reach signal in 2025–2026.** Repeated (vendor-sourced)
  weightings:
  - "1 save = ~5x more reach than 1 like"; "1 save ≈ 2x a meaningful comment"; a save
    gives the author's *next* post ~**80% chance** of appearing in that user's feed;
    saved-post authors see ~130% higher follow likelihood.
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
  - FLAG: these exact multipliers are van der Blom / vendor figures, not LinkedIn-
    published. Directionally everyone agrees: **saves > comments > likes.**
- LinkedIn now treats saves as "deep relevance" and **resurfaces saved posts for 2–3
  weeks** (vs the old ~24h window). **Public save counts rolled out September 2025** —
  saves are now visible on every post.
  - Source: https://getdewey.co/blog/linkedin-2026-algorithm-saves/ ;
    https://medium.com/@_junaidkhalid/how-linkedins-new-saves-feature-finally-reveals-your-content-s-true-impact-september-2025-d433b5bb83ec
- **What executives actually save** (the gold for us):
  - **Frameworks, checklists, templates, step-by-step playbooks, contrarian
    breakdowns, data breakdowns** — content "worth remembering / reusing later."
  - "**Hot takes and personal updates rarely get saved.**"
  - Sources: https://getdewey.co/blog/linkedin-2026-algorithm-saves/ ;
    https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/linkedin-content-strategy-b2b
  - This is the single most actionable finding for an anti-hype expert brand:
    **build the post so a CTO would screenshot/save it.** Reusable structure beats
    performance.

### 3c. Comments — depth over volume, and reply fast

- **Comments weighted ~2x likes** during the engagement-testing phase (some vendors
  say "15x likes" — FLAG that higher figure as inconsistent/unverified).
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
- Meaningful = **15+ word comments** that add insight/ask a real question; one-word
  comments count for little. ("Comment YES" bait is penalized.)
- **Author replies within the first 30 min → +64% total comments and ~2.3x more
  views.** Indirect comments (reply-to-reply threads) can give up to **2.4x reach.**
  - Source: https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-algorithm-explained (van der Blom data)
- **Golden hour:** first **30–60 min** of engagement sets the reach trajectory (3-stage
  model: 0–60 min classification → 1–2 hr engagement test → 2hr+ extended distribution).
  - Source: https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm
- Honest way to get 15+ word comments: end on a question that *requires* the reader's
  own experience to answer; take a clear, defensible position people want to add to or
  push back on; and **show up in the comments to reply substantively** in the first
  half hour.

### 3d. Engagement bait is actively penalized

- "Comment YES if you agree," reaction-polling, follow-bait, and "low-value formatting"
  are **detected and penalized**; one 2026 vendor figure: bait + external links
  penalized ~60%.
  - Sources: https://getdewey.co/blog/linkedin-2026-algorithm-saves/ ;
    https://www.sophieflow.com/article/the-end-of-linkedin-broetry-why-value-driven-ai-storytelling-is-winning-in-2026
  - FLAG: the "60%" is a vendor number. The *fact* that LinkedIn de-prioritizes
    engagement bait is officially stated by LinkedIn (long-standing policy) and
    consistently reported. Safe to treat as real.

## 4. OPENING vs THE REST — first line and second line

- **The first line is ~80% of the job.** It's the only thing guaranteed visible in the
  feed; if it doesn't stop the scroll, the body never gets read. Write it to land
  inside the **mobile ~140-char fold.** (AuthoredUp char-limit source, §1a.)
- **Hook types** that vendors report performing (FLAG: the multipliers below are
  vendor/agency-reported, NOT a controlled study — use as a menu, not as physics):
  - Personal-story hook ("3 years ago I almost quit…") — claimed ~4x engagement.
  - Bold-statement hook ("The LinkedIn advice killing your reach…") — claimed ~2x.
  - Contrarian hook — claimed ~3x.
  - Surprising statistic — high curiosity.
  - Sources: https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-content-hooks-templates ;
    GrackerAI (above). **Treat the numbers as folklore; the hook *categories* are real
    and widely used.**
- **The SECOND line's job (still above the fold):** it must *pay off enough of the hook
  to earn the "see more" click* WITHOUT being a cheap cliffhanger. The "re-hook":
  add the concrete specific, the stake, or the "here's what I learned" promise so the
  reader knows the click is worth it. (GrackerAI re-hook step, §1b.)
- For an anti-hype brand: the honest hook is a **specific, true, slightly surprising
  claim or number** + a second line that states the stake plainly. No fake "I cried in
  my car" theater. Edelman backs surprise/challenge as quality (§5), so a sharp,
  defensible contrarian or "you're measuring X wrong" opener is both honest AND
  algorithm-friendly.

## 5. SUBSTANTIVE vs PERFORMATIVE — can plain, no-gimmick posts win? (yes)

### 5a. The buyer evidence (Edelman-LinkedIn 2025 B2B Thought Leadership Impact Report)

Study of ~**2,000 global B2B professionals**, fielded 17 Mar–3 Apr 2025, 7 markets
(US, CA, UK, DE, SG, AU, IN). Theme: "Invisible Influence / Hidden Buyers."
- Landing: https://www.edelman.com/expertise/Business-Marketing/2025-b2b-thought-leadership-report
- Full PDF: https://www.edelman.com/sites/g/files/aatuss191/files/2025-07/2025%20Edelman-LinkedIn%20B2B%20Thought%20Leadership%20Impact%20Report_FINAL.pdf
- One-pager: https://www.edelman.com/sites/g/files/aatuss191/files/2025-06/2025%20B2B%20Thought%20Leadership%20Impact%20Report%20One%20Pager_FINAL.pdf

Key stats (all support substance over performance):
- **95%** of hidden buyers are more receptive to sales outreach from brands that
  publish strong thought leadership; **79%** more likely to advocate for them in the
  **RFP** process.
- **71%** of decision-makers say thought leadership is **more effective than
  conventional marketing/sales material** at showing a vendor's capability; **64%**
  trust it more than traditional marketing.
- **86%** prefer content that **challenges their assumptions**; **91%** value insights
  that help them uncover challenges/needs they hadn't recognized. → **contrarian,
  problem-revealing substance is what buyers want.**
- **60%** associate a **unique format/style** with high-quality thought leadership.
- **51%** say high-quality TL helps them convince C-level to back a vendor; **52%** to
  persuade other decision-makers.
- **63%** of hidden buyers spend **1+ hour/week** consuming thought leadership;
  **71%** have little/no direct sales contact during evaluation. → the content IS the
  sales motion.
- More than **40%** of B2B deals stall due to internal misalignment (TL is what helps
  internal champions sell up/across).
  - Secondary summary: https://www.contentgrip.com/edelman-linkedin-thought-leadership-report/
  - FLAG: Edelman's own report (well-known caveat across editions) also finds that
    *poor-quality* thought leadership actively damages reputation — i.e. mediocre/
    performative content is worse than none. (Stated in prior Edelman editions and the
    2025 narrative; exact 2025 % not captured in the pages fetched — verify against the
    full PDF before quoting a number.)

### 5b. The platform side: the algorithm now rewards substance

- LinkedIn's stated 2025–2026 direction: reward **dwell time, "knowledge value,"
  meaningful comments**; throttle motivational platitudes and engagement bait; "be a
  professional learning platform, not a diary." Substantive content = "frameworks,
  data, case studies, actionable insights."
  - Source: https://www.sophieflow.com/article/the-end-of-linkedin-broetry-why-value-driven-ai-storytelling-is-winning-in-2026
- Generic AI-slop content is reported to be **actively down-ranked**; "LinkedIn is done
  rewarding surface-level engagement."
  - Source: https://www.zoomsphere.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm-2026-why-generic-ai-content-kills-your-organic-reach
- Educational content (frameworks, data breakdowns, how-to) "consistently outperforms
  promotional content" for B2B; the most valuable real estate is the **founder/exec
  personal profile**, not the company page.
  - Source: https://www.stackmatix.com/blog/linkedin-content-strategy-b2b

### 5c. Verdict

Plain, substantive, no-gimmick posts CAN win — and increasingly are the *only* thing
that wins for a credibility audience — but they win on **dwell + saves + real
comments**, not on raw viral reach. The trade we're making: text posts have the
lowest format reach multiplier (0.88x), so we accept smaller audiences in exchange for
the *right* audience saving and acting. The recipe that satisfies both buyers (Edelman)
and algorithm (van der Blom/SophieFlow):

> A true, slightly-surprising hook (lands in the mobile ~140-char fold) → a second line
> that states the stake plainly → ~1,300–2,000 chars of a *named framework / data
> breakdown / contrarian-but-defensible argument* written at grade ~5–7, in short
> paragraphs (not whole-post one-liners) → maybe one structural emoji and 0–3 hashtags
> → a close that's a real question only an experienced operator can answer → author
> replies substantively within 30 min. Built so a CTO would SAVE it.

## 6. SOURCE QUALITY LEDGER (what to trust)

- **Primary / strongest:**
  - Edelman-LinkedIn 2025 B2B TL Impact Report (~2,000 buyers, named method, PDF).
  - AuthoredUp data studies: 372,126 posts (length, Sept25–Feb26) and 847,000+ posts
    (emoji). Observational but large, recent, method stated.
  - Richard van der Blom Algorithm Insights 2025 (1.8M posts). The de-facto standard;
    but it's a practitioner report, much of it relayed via secondary blogs — verify
    specific multipliers against the actual report chapters before quoting.
- **Useful framing, treat structure-not-numbers:** GrackerAI, SophieFlow, dewey,
  stackmatix, meet-lea, magicpost — agency/vendor blogs. Good on *categories and
  consensus*; their precise multipliers (4x hook, 60% link penalty, 15x comment) are
  unverified folklore — flagged inline throughout.
- **Cultural/critique sources (broetry):** CMI, Medium/txsling, fenwick.media — useful
  for *why* broetry is now a credibility liability.

### Folklore flagged in this doc (do NOT state as fact without hedging)
- Hook multipliers (4x story / 3x contrarian / 2x bold).
- "1 save = 5x a like," "save → 80% next-post visibility," "130% follow lift."
- "Comments 15x likes" (inconsistent with the more-cited 2x).
- "Above 10th grade = −35% reach."
- "4+ links = 3–5x reach" (almost certainly an artifact — ignore).
- "External links/bait penalized 60%."
- "Algorithm specifically penalizes broetry formatting" (the bait penalty is real; the
  broetry-formatting penalty specifically is asserted, not proven).
