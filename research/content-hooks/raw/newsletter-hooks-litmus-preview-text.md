# Litmus — The Ultimate Guide to Preview Text

Source: https://www.litmus.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-preview-text-support
Accessed: 2026-06-19

## Character Limits
- Keep preview text under 90 characters (best practice).
- Display varies: subscribers can see anywhere from "5 lines of preview text (about 278 characters) to 0 lines."
- Email clients allow user customization → exact limits unpredictable.
- Place "the most important part near or at the beginning so your message comes across—even if it gets cut off."

## Subject Line vs Preview Text Strategy
- Preview text should COMPLEMENT rather than repeat the subject line.
- "Think of the preview text as an extension of the subject line."
- If subject hooks with urgency/curiosity, use preview to "add more context" or supporting details.
- Chubbies makes preview complete the subject "so it reads like one sentence altogether."
- Avoid "recycling the same copy" — repetition is "a missed second chance at reeling your subscribers in."

## Concrete Examples
Complementary:
- Subject: "Winter sale: Hundreds of styles up to 40% off"
- Preview: "Plus, some stories to get you out there"
Avoiding repetition:
- Subject: "50% off new arrivals" / Preview: explain what merchandise type arrived (not repeat the discount)

## ANALYST NOTE
Key actionable rules for the brand: (1) never let preview text auto-pull the first body line by default; (2) front-load meaning in first ~40-50 chars; (3) use preview to extend, not echo, the subject — i.e., subject states the topic, preview adds the specific payoff or the one-line "why it matters."
