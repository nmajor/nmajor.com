# Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) — human-review provision, but SUPERSEDED before taking effect

Captured: 2026-07-07

## What SB 24-205 originally required
URL: https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb24-205
- Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205), signed 2024. Applied to "consequential decisions," defined to
  include insurance, financial/lending, employment, housing, healthcare, education, legal services.
- For a high-risk AI system making/substantially influencing an adverse consequential decision,
  the deployer had to give the consumer an opportunity to **appeal, with human review "if
  technically feasible."** Plus disclosure + a duty of reasonable care to avoid algorithmic
  discrimination.
- Insurance carve-out: an insurer already subject to the CO Division of Insurance's AI rules
  (3 CCR 702-10) was deemed in compliance.

## The catch (CRITICAL — this example is now weak)
- Effective date was delayed twice: originally Feb 1, 2026 -> pushed to **June 30, 2026** by
  SB 25B-004 (signed Aug 28, 2025).
- Then, before it ever took effect, the legislature **repealed and replaced** the framework via
  **SB 26-189**, which drops the affirmative anti-discrimination duty and the human-review appeal
  and replaces it with a lighter **disclosure** regime; new framework effective **Jan 1, 2027**.
- So: SB 24-205's "human review of an adverse AI decision" mandate **never became operative.**

## Assessment
DO NOT cite Colorado SB 205 as a live "regulator requires a human in the loop" example. Its human-
review appeal provision was delayed and then repealed before ever taking effect (replaced by a
disclosure-only regime, SB 26-189, effective Jan 1, 2027). Using it as current law would be a
factual error the essay's honesty brand can't afford. If mentioned at all, frame it as the
cautionary case: the most-hyped US state "AI civil rights" law was walked back before it bit.
