# Jonah Berger, *Contagious: Why Things Catch On* (2013) — the STEPPS framework

Sources:
- Knowledge@Wharton (Berger, primary interview): https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/contagious-jonah-berger-on-why-things-catch-on
- Berger's own "Crafting Contagious" workbook (primary PDF): https://jonahberger.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Crafting-Contagious-Workbook.pdf
- Havas Market synthesis: https://www.havasmarket.co.uk/stepps-framework-shareability

Book: Jonah Berger, *Contagious: Why Things Catch On*, Simon & Schuster, 2013.
STEPPS = six drivers of word-of-mouth / sharing. Berger's thesis: "virality isn't
luck — it's engineered." Follows predictable principles, not chance.

## The six STEPPS (verbatim from Berger's workbook + Wharton interview)

1. **Social Currency** — "Status by association. People like to look smart, funny,
   and in-the-know, so they talk about things that help them portray a positive
   image." "It's all about people talking about things to make themselves look
   good, rather than bad." People share what makes THEM look good. (This is the
   engine of RESHARING specifically — see file 03.)

2. **Triggers** — "Top of mind, tip of tongue." "People talk about things that are
   top-of-mind. Using subtle reminders to help them think about your idea will make
   them more likely to share." Things linked to frequent cues in daily life get
   talked about more often.

3. **Emotion** — "When we care, we share. High arousal emotions — like excitement,
   anger, and awe — fire people up. This activation, in turn, drives them to share."
   (Direct application of Berger & Milkman 2012, file 01. Havas summary: "aim for a
   clear emotional spike rather than a vaguely 'nice' piece... lukewarm rarely
   travels.")

4. **Public** — "Built to show, built to grow. People often imitate others. But you
   can't imitate what you can't observe. Making behavior more public enables social
   influence." Observability drives imitation.

5. **Practical Value** — "News you can use. People share information to help others,
   to make them better off." Useful tips/hacks/advice get shared because sharing
   them positions the sharer as helpful and knowledgeable.

6. **Stories** — "People share things that are often wrapped up in stories or
   narratives." "Information travels under the guise of idle chatter." Berger's
   Trojan Horse metaphor: build an outer story that carries social currency /
   practical value, and "hide" the message/brand inside it so it rides along.

## Berger's "Trojan Horse" warning (Wharton interview, verbatim)
"The more you brand something, the more it looks like an ad, the less willing people
are going to be to talk about and share it. You need to design content that's like a
Trojan horse. There's an exterior that's really exciting, remarkable and has social
currency or practical value. But inside, you hide the brand..."
→ For nmajor.com: the "hard sell" (a checklist to gate a lead, a framework that
reads as a product pitch) suppresses sharing; a genuinely arresting STORY that
happens to carry the point travels.

## Relevance to the breakout question
STEPPS is a checklist for shareability. Nick's breakout post (Post A) hits at least
four of six strongly: **Stories** (it is a narrative), **Emotion** (anxiety/awe —
high arousal), **Social currency** (resharing "I saw this / you need to see this"

governance lesson). The checklist post (Post B) hits only **Practical value** — one
of six, and the least activating one. STEPPS predicts the gap.
