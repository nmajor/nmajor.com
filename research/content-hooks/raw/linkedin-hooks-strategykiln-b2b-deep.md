## Classic copywriting principles (credible heritage)
- David Ogilvy — "Open with Fire": start with drama and immediacy.
- John Caples — "The success of an entire advertising campaign may stand or fall on what is said in the headlines."
- Joseph Sugarman — "the sole purpose of the first sentence is to get the reader to read the second sentence." (the "slippery slide")
- AIDA model (Attention–Interest–Desire–Action): Attention first.

## Psychological triggers
- Curiosity gaps and the **Zeigarnik effect** (uncompleted tasks create compulsion to resolve).
- Emotional arousal (high-arousal emotions increase sharing).
- Relevance and self-interest filtering.
- Novelty and surprise as attention mechanisms.
- Specificity and credibility through data/numbers.

## STEPPS framework (Jonah Berger) adapted to hooks
1. Social Currency – insider knowledge sharing
2. Triggers – top-of-mind environmental cues
3. Emotion – high-arousal feeling states
4. Public – bandwagon/FOMO signals
5. Practical Value – actionable, useful content
6. Stories – narrative vessels for ideas

## Text-post hook strategy
- Keep under ~210 characters before "See more" cutoff (1–3 lines).
- Use white space and line breaks for scanning.
- Start with bold/contrarian statements.
- End on cliffhangers (colons, ellipses, questions).
- Call out target audience directly (roles, industries).

## Carousel / image / video hook notes
- Image and text hooks should complement each other; first carousel slide = visual cover.
- Bold typography, high contrast for mobile.
- Caption opening lines carry separate hooking weight.
- Video: hook within 1–2 seconds; on-screen captions (auto-play muted); pattern interrupts; "rehook" ~30s mark; address pain points early.

## Influencer hook patterns (named creators)
**Justin Welsh:**
- "Relatable Enemy > Hero > Teaser" sequence
- Numbered list template: "X lessons I wish I knew before I [did Y]"
- Negative qualifiers in parentheses

**Jasmin Alić:**
- Story-driven "Rude Awakening" openings
- "Rehook" concept: mid-post curiosity reinforcement
- "A hook is 90% of a good post… but if the rest doesn't follow the same energy, it falls apart"

**Jake Ward:**
- Outcome-first approach highlighting impressive results
- Specific numbers and timeframes; success-story angle with tangible metrics

**Nat Berman:**
- Direct pain-point identification; do/don't contrasts; value-first, no-fluff

## B2B-specific hook advice (HIGHLY RELEVANT to anti-hype, decision-maker audience)
- Personalize hooks for specific customer personas.
- Reference industry trends / current triggers top-of-mind for professionals.
- Use business outcomes (revenue, KPIs, ROI) rather than generic benefits.
- Mention roles (CEO, PM, Sales Lead) to signal relevance.
- Avoid industry jargon unless it genuinely resonates.
- Emphasize metrics and concrete results over abstract concepts.
- Professional emotions matter: pride, frustration, ambition, FOMO.

## Data & performance insights
- Only ~3 lines (200–220 characters) display "above the fold" before "See more."
- First line is "more important than 95% of the rest of the post."
- High-arousal emotions correlate with increased sharing/virality.
- Numbers, timeframes, recognizable entities boost hook performance.
- Specific details add credibility AND intrigue.

## Credibility & avoiding clickbait (CRITICAL — aligns with anti-hype brand)
Core principle: "A hook is only as good as the content that follows it" — ethical hooking requires matching energy and delivering on promises.

Trust-building practices:
- Align hook and content; avoid bait-and-switch.
- Provide unique value, fresh data, or experienced perspectives.
- **Temper hyperbole; use phrases like "made me question" instead of grand prophecies.**
- Verify numbers and facts; be prepared to source claims.
- Close curiosity loops established by the hook.
- Maintain accuracy; consistent honesty strengthens future hook effectiveness.
- "Think of your hook as a service to the reader."

Against sensationalism:
- "In B2B contexts, credibility is paramount. Reframe attention-grabbing language to remain professional and authentic rather than manipulative."
