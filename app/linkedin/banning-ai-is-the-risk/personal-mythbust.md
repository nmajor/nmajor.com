Ban AI at work and you do not remove the risk. You lose sight of it.

I understand why leaders reach for a ban. Customer data in a chatbot is a real fear, and "no AI" feels like the safe call. It is not.

Your people are already using AI, whether you bought it or not. An MIT study this year found roughly 90% of employees use personal AI tools for work, even where the company never bought an official one. Forbid the official tool and the work does not stop. It moves to personal accounts, on personal phones, with no rules, no logs, and no way for you to know what went where.

That is the trade a ban actually makes. You swap a tool you could govern for one you cannot see.

CB Financial, a Pennsylvania bank, found this out the expensive way. An employee ran customer records through an unapproved AI tool, and it became an SEC 8-K filing. The fix was never "ban AI." It was to give people a sanctioned tool and one clear rule, so the easy path and the safe path are the same path.

So the goal is not less AI. It is AI you can see.

Pick a paid account that will not train on your data, make it the official one, and write a single rule everyone understands: customer records never go into a personal AI account.

That is more security than any ban will buy you, because people will actually follow it.

If you banned AI at your company, how sure are you that it stopped?
