# Raw capture — UnitedHealth's own response / defense on nH Predict (fetched 2026-07-07)

## The company's core characterization (for balance)
UnitedHealth spokesperson, emailed statement (reported across STAT, ArentFox Schiff, Medical Economics, CBS News):
> "The **naviHealth predict tool is not used to make coverage determinations.** The tool is used as a guide to help us inform providers, families and other caregivers about what sort of assistance and care the patient may need both in the facility and after returning home. **Coverage decisions are based on CMS coverage criteria and the terms of the member's plan.**"

## The contested core (the whole dispute in one line)
- **UnitedHealth's position:** nH Predict is a *decision-support / planning guide*; humans (applying CMS criteria + plan terms) make the actual coverage calls.
- **Plaintiffs' allegation (Lokken):** UHC used nH Predict **"in lieu of physicians"** to make the coverage determinations, and pressured staff (the "within 1% of projected days" target) to conform to the algorithm — i.e., de facto the algorithm decided.
- The Feb 13, 2025 ruling let the breach-of-contract / good-faith claims proceed precisely because the court found plaintiffs plausibly alleged AI was used "in lieu of physicians." **The factual question of nH Predict's actual role is unresolved and is exactly what the March 2026 discovery order aims to illuminate.**

## Framing note for the essay (anti-hype discipline)
- Keep the two systems distinct: (a) **nH Predict** — older predictive/case-matching model (subject of Lokken + the PSI report), NOT an LLM; (b) UnitedHealth's **2026 generative-AI push** ($1.5B/yr; "2:1 return"; Optum Real) — a separate, current program. The litigation is about (a); the earnings story is about (b). Don't let one contaminate the other.
- Nothing here is a proven finding of wrongful denial by AI. As of mid-2026: two contract-based claims survive, class not yet certified, broad discovery ordered. That is the honest state of play.
