Interview with Peter Zaffino, executive chairman of AIG, on the Palantir deployment.

Portfolio analysis timeline:
- Before: 30-to-90 day lag on portfolio analysis.
- After: daily updates, enabling real-time risk management across commercial underwriting.

Everest acquisition integration:
- Premium book size: $2 billion.
- Integration timeline: 4 days (versus months previously).
- Method: layered Everest's portfolio onto AIG's existing Palantir ontology.

Technology and approach:
- Ontology (digital twin) functions as the infrastructure; LLMs extract data and accelerate
  workflows, but the ontology enables agent direction and portfolio-wide decision tracking.
- "Having the ability to assess risk and use quantitative data to make better decisions on
  a daily basis is the aspiration."
- Data architecture finding: the team discovered centralized data lakes weren't necessary;
  routing directly to admin platforms proved faster and sufficient.

Deployment model:
- Palantir forward-deployed engineers work on-site with AIG teams.
- 90-day iteration cycles rather than annual roadmaps; shared business outcomes agreed before each cycle.

Workforce:
- Zaffino rejects the layoff characterization, framing change as eliminating "processes where
  humans have been functioning as manual LLMs"; emphasis on growth and reskilling.
- No specific employee figures or dates provided in this piece.

Corroborating context (Exa, treat as secondary / actuary.info is a niche analyst blog):
- AIG also deployed Anthropic's Claude internally to underwriters; partners with Palantir and Salesforce
  on "AIG Underwriter Companion." AIG reportedly says underwriters spend ~50%+ less time on data
  ingestion and submission triage (getperspective.ai blog — vendor-adjacent, unverified).
- "AIG Assist" cited at 40% binding lift across eight lines, Q1 2026 earnings call (actuary.info — niche blog).
