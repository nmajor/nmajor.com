## Source: Newsletter Operator — ADAPT framework
URL: https://www.newsletteroperator.com/p/adapt-framework-5-ways-to-repurpose-your-newsletter

ADAPT Framework (5 ways to repurpose a newsletter into 500+ posts/year):
- **Amplify**: "Expand a paragraph, section, or idea into a full post that goes deeper." Take deeper concepts from longer newsletters and create standalone social posts expanding single ideas.
- **Direct**: "Direct adaptations of the content to social." Summarize main newsletter content into formats like LinkedIn carousels, Instagram posts, or Twitter threads.
- **Ask**: "Share question, start a debate, or poll to drive engagement." e.g. poll "Do you repurpose your newsletter into other content?" or open-ended questions.
- **Pull**: "Pull out and post a short quote or section from your newsletter." Extract memorable one-liners/paragraphs as standalone posts, optionally in graphics.
- **Transcribe**: "Create script for a video and/or podcast." Convert newsletters into video scripts or podcast content, AI for spoken adaptation.

Scale math claimed: "a weekly newsletter and repurpose it into 5 posts per week on 2 platforms" = "500+ posts per year."

## Source: Melanie Goodman — The LinkedIn↔Substack Repurposing Flywheel
URL: https://melaniegoodmanlinkedinconsultant.substack.com/p/the-linkedin-substack-repurposing-flywheel

Four-part system (the "Flywheel"):
- **Source**: original Substack/newsletter post
- **Slice**: break into 3–5 angles
- **Shape**: format natively for LinkedIn
- **Signal**: drive readers back to Substack/newsletter

Number of posts: One newsletter generates **3–5 LinkedIn posts over 1–2 weeks.**

Four "Slice" types → LinkedIn formats:
1. Personal Story/Example → Narrative post "Hook → Turning point → Insight"
2. Myth-bust/Counterintuitive Insight → Contrarian opener format
3. Micro-framework/How-to → List-style post or carousel
4. Common Mistake/Pattern to Avoid → "Don't do this" post with reframe

Selection criteria — start with "proven winners" from last 90 days:
- High open rates, clicks, or forwards
- Strong reader reactions
- Posts triggering replies or client inquiries
- Clear problem-solution structure

Weekly cadence example:
- Day 1: Publish newsletter
- Day 2: Post story angle
- Day 4: Post framework/checklist angle
- Day 6: Post myth-bust with "gentle Substack reference"

CTA strategy:
- Soft-touch CTA: "If you want the full breakdown, I covered it in last week's newsletter."
- Place links in first comments. [NOTE: see link-in-comments raw file — this advice is contested/dated for 2026.]
- For every 3–4 value-only posts, share 1 newsletter-referencing post. Maintains reach + builds curiosity-driven subscribers.

## Source: General atomization principles (search synthesis, multiple SEO/tool blogs)
URLs: https://www.trykondo.com/blog/linkedin-content-strategy-b2b ; https://bluetext.com/blog/the-content-atomization-playbook-one-idea-dozens-of-deliverables/ ; https://www.frase.io/features/content-atomization ; https://postiv.ai/blog/content-repurposing-strategies

- "Pillar-to-Post" method: deep authority via comprehensive articles + daily engagement via shorter formats (text, carousels, video).
- LinkedIn is a single-idea platform; aim for **3–5 angles per newsletter**. Each post must stand alone (no context required).
- A pillar is "repurpose-ready" if it has 3–7 distinct points (framework/steps/checklist/opinion), 1–3 stories/examples, 1–2 data points.
- Format options: narrative Hook→Turning point→Insight; frameworks→list posts/carousels; myth-busting contrarian openers; step-by-step tutorials/checklists.
- Output scale claim: one long-form piece → "5 to 15 LinkedIn posts"; a 2,000-word blog → "3 text posts, 2 carousels, a few shorter insight posts." [Vendor blog claim, no data.]
- Term origin: "Content atomization" credited to Bryan Kramer. Gary Vaynerchuk's model often cited: one keynote → 30+ pieces. [Folklore-adjacent; the GaryVee number is anecdotal.]
- COPE = "Create Once, Publish Everywhere" (older framework, originated at NPR). Source: https://www.postquick.ai/blog/content-repurpose-workflow-for-social-media-create-once-post-everywhere
