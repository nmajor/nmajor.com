# Clickbait A/B test (Perkbox) + Curiosity / Information-Gap Theory

## SOURCE 1: "Do clickbait subject lines work?" — Kayla Medica (Substack)
Source: https://mehdeeka.substack.com/p/do-clickbait-subject-lines-work
Accessed: 2026-06-19
- A/B test (Perkbox) comparing corporate vs clickbait/funny subject lines.
- Highest OPEN rate: Funny/clickbait subject line.
- Highest CLICK-THROUGH rate: Corporate (straightforward) subject line.
- Highest perk REDEMPTION (conversion): Corporate subject line.
- Clickbait example: "Oops, there's an error with your account" (the "error" was just that users hadn't redeemed a perk).
- Key insight: "while the funny one did get more people to open the email, it didn't spur them to action." Corporate version won on the metrics that matter.
- Caveat: "every audience is unique and so you should never assume what the result will be."

## SOURCE 2: Loewenstein Information-Gap Theory of Curiosity (academic)
Sources:
- https://www.cmu.edu/dietrich/sds/docs/golman/golman_loewenstein_curiosity.pdf
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627315007679
Accessed: 2026-06-19
- Loewenstein: curiosity is "a cognitive induced deprivation that arises from the perception of a gap in knowledge and understanding." Functions like a drive state (e.g., hunger).
- An information gap = a question the person is AWARE of (with a set of possible answers) but uncertain about. The bigger the gap on a topic of interest, the stronger the motivation to fill it.
- Specific curiosity = desire to obtain missing info, accompanied by negative emotion from awareness of what one doesn't know.
- Copywriting application: the gap opens when a question gets activated in the reader's mind; the headline must name a real, relevant question and promise genuine new information.

## ANALYST NOTE (critical for brand)
This reconciles "curiosity works" with "clickbait fails":
- Honest curiosity gap = reader must already KNOW enough to feel the specific gap, and the email must FILL it. The gap is real and gets closed.
- Clickbait = a vague/withheld gap ("you won't believe...") that manufactures deprivation and often doesn't deliver. It wins the open but loses click/conversion/trust (Perkbox A/B confirms: opens up, action down).
- For skeptical CTOs/operators: make the gap SPECIFIC and TOPICAL (e.g., "Why our RAG eval scores dropped after the model upgrade") so the curiosity is earned by relevance, and always close the loop in the body.
