The problem:
- 45 days to generate a sales quote for digital pathology customers.
- "Forty-five days of chasing systems, digging through Excel sheets, losing momentum."

The solution: "Sensai"
- New timeline: minutes instead of 45 days.
- Team: fewer than 20 people, cross-functional (Philips, AWS, EPAM).
- Build: under five months total. Prototype in 2 weeks; first PoC in 6 weeks; production under 5 months.
- 75% faster development cycle vs traditional approaches.

Tech stack:
- Amazon Bedrock with agentic capabilities.
- Kiro (AWS AI-powered development environment). "Using AI to build AI."

What Sensai does — three agent types:
1. Pricing agents (configuration and quoting).
2. Coding agents (product customization).
3. Manual-activity orchestrator (CRM updates, quote distribution, email drafting).

Business impact:
- One seller said they'd accept a doubled quota in exchange for the tool (anecdote, not a metric).

Governance:
- Built-in standards from day one: accessibility, security, logging, UX consistency.

Caveat: "45 days to minutes" is the quoting-cycle time for one product line (digital pathology),
told from the stage. No volume, dollar, or post-launch adoption figures. AWS event = promotional context.
Philips is a 135-year-old company.
