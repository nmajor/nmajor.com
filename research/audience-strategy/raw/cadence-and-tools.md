## Realistic weekly operating rhythm (part-time creator)
URLs: https://blog.annabyang.com/content-production-solopreneur/ ; https://solopreneurcode.substack.com/p/solopreneur-system-content-production ; https://pursuenetworking.com/blog/linkedin-content-workflow/ ; https://cindybidar.com/my-complete-content-publishing-system-for-2026/
- Batching workflow example: Mon = capture 5–10 ideas; Tue = engage with others' posts; Wed = draft 5–7 posts in 90 min; Thu = edit/polish 60 min; Fri = review scheduled posts + engage with network.
- Time investment: launch 5–8 hrs/week → month 6 ~3–5 hrs/week → month 12 ~2–3 hrs/week (via batching/systems).
- Newsletter batching: set aside ~4 hrs MONTHLY to write 4–8 weeks of issues, batch in Notion/Google Docs, schedule across calendar.
- Core principle: separate MECHANICAL parts (formatting, scheduling) from CREATIVE parts (ideation, drafting). Break week into ideation → drafting → polishing → distribution.
- "One post can become a newsletter, a thread, a LinkedIn post, and a lead magnet" from one structured system.

### Synthesized weekly rhythm tying NEWSLETTER + LinkedIn (anti-hype, part-time)
(Aligns with Melanie Goodman Flywheel cadence + algorithm 2–4 posts/week + value-first)
- The newsletter issue is the weekly PILLAR (full article on site + email/Buttondown).
- From each issue, atomize 3 LinkedIn posts (algorithm sweet spot is 2–4/week; 3 is sustainable part-time and avoids the daily-posting -26% reach penalty):
  - Post A (issue day or +1): the standalone STORY/insight angle — full value, soft footer link.
  - Post B (+2–3 days): the FRAMEWORK/checklist angle — ideal as a document/PDF carousel.
  - Post C (+4–5 days): the MYTH-BUST/contrarian angle (on-brand anti-hype) with newsletter reference.
- Engage (comment on others) 2–3x/week — distribution channel per practitioners.
- Batch monthly: write/queue issues; weekly: ~90 min to draft+schedule the 3 posts + build 1 carousel.
- Keep topic-consistent (topic authority compounds; LinkedIn's own tactic #2).

## Scheduling tools
URLs: https://www.supergrow.ai/blog/linkedin-scheduling-tools ; https://postking.io/blog/best-linkedin-scheduling-tools-comparison ; https://carouselli.com/blog/best-ai-tools-for-linkedin
- **Taplio** — leading solo-creator tool; native support for carousels, polls, DOCUMENT posts (drag-drop PDF, mobile preview). Best for personal branding.
- **Publer** — also supports LinkedIn document/carousel post scheduling.
- **Buffer** — free tier (3 posts/mo) but does NOT fully support LinkedIn document/carousel scheduling → carousels must be posted manually.
- **FeedHive, SocialPilot** — bridge design + automated distribution.
- Carousel creation tools: Carouselli, PostNitro, AuthoredUp (also analytics + post formatting/preview).
- KEY GOTCHA: document/PDF carousels are NOT supported by every scheduler — confirm native document-post scheduling before committing (Taplio/Publer = yes; Buffer = no). LinkedIn's own native scheduler also does NOT support scheduling document posts reliably.

## ON-BRAND vs AVOID
- ON-BRAND: scheduling for consistency (LinkedIn tactic #3), batching to protect a part-time founder's time, native posting where it aids reach.
- AVOID: full automation / auto-DM bots / engagement pods (against LinkedIn ToS and off-brand for an honest enterprise positioning).
