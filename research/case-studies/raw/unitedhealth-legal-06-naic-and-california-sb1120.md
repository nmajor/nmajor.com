# Raw capture — NAIC AI model bulletin + California SB 1120 (fetched 2026-07-07)

## NAIC Model Bulletin: Use of Artificial Intelligence Systems by Insurers
- Adopted by the NAIC on **December 4, 2023.** It is a **model bulletin** (guidance/expectation-setting), not a model law — it tells insurers that existing market-conduct/unfair-trade-practice laws apply to AI decisions and requires an AI governance program, risk management, and third-party/vendor oversight.
- **State adoption: ~24 states** had adopted it (with little/no material change) as of **March 2025** (Wisconsin was the 24th, March 18/19, 2025; Alaska first, Feb 1, 2024). ~half of states.
  - Quarles: "Nearly Half of States Have Now Adopted NAIC Model Bulletin on Insurers' Use of AI" https://www.quarles.com/newsroom/publications/nearly-half-of-states-have-now-adopted-naic-model-bulletin-on-insurers-use-of-ai
  - InsureReinsure (Wisconsin = 24th): https://www.insurereinsure.com/2025/03/19/wisconsin-becomes-the-24th-state-to-adopt-the-naic-model-bulletin-on-the-use-of-ais-in-insurance/
  - NAIC official adoption map: https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/legal-adoption-map-ai-model-bulletin.pdf
  - NAIC AI topic hub: https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/artificial-intelligence
- Caveat for honest framing: the NAIC bulletin is **soft law** — it sets governance expectations and reiterates that AI-driven decisions can't violate existing unfair-practices statutes; it does NOT by itself mandate a human decision-maker on every denial. State adoption count (~24) is REPORTED and moving; verify current number before citing.

## California SB 1120 — "Physicians Make Decisions Act" (the hard human-in-the-loop law)
- Author: State Sen. Josh Becker. **Signed by Gov. Newsom September 2024; effective January 1, 2025.**
- What it requires (health plans/insurers doing utilization review in CA):
  - A **denial, delay, or modification** of care based on medical necessity **must be made by a licensed physician or other licensed health care professional** competent to evaluate the specific clinical issues — AI/algorithms **may not supplant** provider decision-making.
  - AI/algorithm tools used in utilization review must base determinations on the **enrollee's own medical history and individual clinical circumstances**, not "generalized datasets"; must be fairly and equitably applied; and must NOT be the **sole basis** for denying/delaying/modifying care.
  - Subject to audit/compliance review by CA DMHC and Dept. of Insurance; willful violations carry administrative penalties.
- Sources:
  - Sen. Becker (Sept 30, 2024 signing): https://sd13.senate.ca.gov/news/press-release/september-30-2024/governor-signs-physicians-make-decisions-act-keeping-medical
  - Fenwick analysis: https://www.fenwick.com/insights/publications/californias-sb-1120-regulates-ai-in-health-plan-utilization-review-and-management-activities-starting-in-january

## Net: the guardrails that make human-in-the-loop a *legal* requirement (not a nicety)
- **CMS-4201-F + Feb 2024 FAQ** (federal, Medicare Advantage): algorithm can't be sole basis for post-acute termination or inpatient denial; individualized medical-necessity determination required. [HARD, federal, effective 2024]
- **California SB 1120** (state, all CA health plans): licensed clinician must make the medical-necessity denial; AI can't be sole basis. [HARD, state law, effective 2025]
- **NAIC AI Model Bulletin** (~24 states): governance/accountability expectations; existing unfair-practices law applies to AI. [SOFT guidance, adopted piecemeal]
