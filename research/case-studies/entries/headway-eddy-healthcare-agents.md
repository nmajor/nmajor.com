## What they did
Arnaud Ferreri, CTO of Headway (a company building infrastructure for accessible mental
healthcare), writes that the team built its own internal AI agent, "Eddy," rather than
buying an off-the-shelf agent platform, on the argument that healthcare-safe handling of
data and compliance was the differentiator they could not outsource.

## The numbers
- Before: not framed as a paid-SaaS replacement; the choice was build vs buy an agent platform.
- After: Eddy, an internally built agent, used for day-to-day engineering work.
- Cost / spend: no before/after dollar figures given.
- Time / headcount / volume: Eddy was built "in the last two months"; the CTO says he
  "merged 60 pull requests" in a month, "most written by Eddy," a pace his calendar had not
  allowed in years.

## What broke / the caveats
This is a first-person engineering-blog post on Medium, so it is self-reported and reads as
advocacy for the build decision; there are no cost figures and no independent verification.
The concrete detail is velocity (60 PRs/month, two-month build), not money. The "healthcare
needs are special" framing is a common reason teams give for building, and it is asserted
here rather than demonstrated with outcomes.

## Why it matters for our beat
A named healthcare company making an explicit build-vs-buy call for AI agents, with
compliance/data-safety as the stated reason to build, is a useful regulated-industry data
point, distinct from the cost-driven SaaS-replacement stories.
