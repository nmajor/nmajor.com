# Kant — Formula of Humanity / Dignity (1785)

**Citation:** Immanuel Kant, *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals* (1785). Standard
Akademie pagination: the Formula of Humanity at 4:429; the dignity/"price vs. dignity"
passage at 4:434–435.

**Sources:**
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kantian_ethics
- https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/kant-moral/ (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)

## The standard formulations (verbatim, standard English renderings)

**Formula of Humanity (2nd formulation of the Categorical Imperative):**
"So act that you use humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other,
always at the same time as an end, never merely as a means." (Groundwork 4:429)

**Dignity vs. price:**
"What has a price can be replaced by something else as its equivalent; what on the other
hand is raised above all price and therefore admits of no equivalent has a dignity."
(Groundwork 4:434) A person has dignity — is "beyond price" and irreplaceable — whereas a
thing has a price.

**Critical word:** "merely." Kant forbids treating a person *merely* as a means (as if that
is all they are), not any instrumental use whatsoever.

## Mapping to Nick's thesis (PARTIAL-to-STRONG, if framed carefully)

The objection to reducing a sick individual to a **statistical prediction matched against a
database of other patients** is Kantian at its core: it treats the person *merely* as a data
point / a means to an actuarial end, not as an irreplaceable end in themselves with a
particular body and case. nH Predict's alleged method — "matching their circumstances to
similar patients ... rather than deferring to a human physician's medical judgment" — is
almost a caricature of treating the person as a fungible instance rather than an end.

"accountability can't be automated" claim (Kant is about how to treat persons, not directly
