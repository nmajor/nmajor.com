## What they did
Atonom, a 25-to-30-person startup that sells AI sales and support agents, dropped its
Salesforce contract and rebuilt its CRM on Lovable's AI app-building platform. Jason, the
head of finance and legal, stood up a working prototype in hours. The CRM handles lead
capture, pipeline and opportunity tracking, ARR/MRR reporting, dashboards, and weekly
sales-meeting reports, and integrates with Atonom's own AI sales agent so form submissions
auto-trigger outreach. The team went on to build finance and forecasting tools the same way.

## The numbers
- Before: $40,000/year Salesforce contract for 25-30 seats.
- After: ~$1,200/year including hosting; also removed the need for a dedicated CRM admin.
- Cost / spend: stated ~97% reduction in annual CRM software cost (vendor-reported).
- Time / headcount / volume: prototype built "within hours"; one finance/legal lead, not
  an engineer, did the build.

## What broke / the caveats
The numbers trace to a single source: Lovable's own blog post about a customer. Analytics
India, TechGig, and Fraction all re-report that vendor case study rather than verifying it
independently, so the $40k-to-$1,200 figure is vendor-promoted marketing. The company and
CRO Gabe Larsen ("It was 40 grand a year for 25 or 30 people... this is crazy for the
functionality we need") are consistently named, but no independent audit of the savings or
of how the CRM held up over time exists in these sources.
