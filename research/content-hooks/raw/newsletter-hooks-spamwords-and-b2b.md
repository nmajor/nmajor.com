# Spam-trigger / hype words to avoid + B2B subject-line guidance

## SOURCE 1: Spam-filter trigger words (SpamAssassin rules)
Source: https://martech.zone/subject-line-words-spam/
Accessed: 2026-06-19
Words/phrases that hurt deliverability or read as spam (AVOID — also overlaps with the brand's banned hype words):
- Urgency & action: "Act Now," "URGENT," "Click Here"
- Financial promises: "Double Your Income," "Earn Money," "Increase Sales," "Lowest Price," "Best Price," "Save Big," "Save Up to," "Double Your"
- Deceptive offers: "FREE," "Bonus," "Guarantee," "Risk-Free," "Zero Risk," "No Obligation," "No Fees," "No Credit Check"
- Scam-related: "Investment Opportunity," "Work From Home," "Debt Free," "Multi-Level Marketing"
- Health/weight: "Lose Weight," "Miracle"
- Prize/winner: "WINNER," "You Are a Winner," "Congratulations"
- Promotional: "Buy Now," "Affordable," "Exclusive Deal," "Hot Deals," "Instant Access," "Instant Approval," "Special Promotion," "Trial Offer," "Unlimited," "Luxury"
- ALL CAPS and excessive punctuation (!!!) also flagged.

## SOURCE 2: B2B subject lines for time-pressed executives (search synthesis)
Sources: beehiiv, theleadcrafters, averi.ai, prospeo
Accessed: 2026-06-19
- Specificity wins: "Your Q1 content benchmarks are out" beats generic "This week's newsletter."
- Generic subjects ("This week's newsletter") consistently underperform.
- Concrete outcomes: "Cut down reporting time from hours to minutes," "Cut compliance costs in half" (segment-targeted).
- B2B newsletter open-rate benchmark: ~15-25% industry average.
- "47% of email recipients decide whether to open based solely on the subject line."
- Key: "executives, managers, and business owners are pressed for time, so they're more likely to engage with concise, relevant, and professional messages." Consumer-marketing tactics don't transfer.

## ANALYST NOTE
This is the strongest practical pairing for the brand:
- DO: be specific + relevant to the reader's actual situation; concise; professional; sentence case.
- DON'T: use any of the SpamAssassin words above — most are exactly the "hype words / manufactured emphasis" the brand already bans. Avoiding them simultaneously protects deliverability AND stays on-voice.
- The "Your Q1 benchmarks are out" pattern is the B2B analog of VerticalResponse's "your 2025 pricing summary" — plain, specific, useful, human.
