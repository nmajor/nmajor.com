Title: **"Refusal of Recovery: How Medicare Advantage Insurers Have Denied Patients Access to Post-Acute Care."** U.S. Senate Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations (PSI), Majority staff, Chair **Sen. Richard Blumenthal (D-CT)**. Released **October 17, 2024**. ~54 pages. Based on **>280,000 pages** of documents from UnitedHealthcare, Humana, CVS (three insurers covering ~60% of MA enrollees; investigation launched May 17, 2023).

### VERBATIM — post-acute care overall denial rate (the 8.7% → 22.7% figure)
> "In 2019, UnitedHealthcare issued an initial denial to **8.7 percent** of the post-acute care prior authorization requests it received; by 2022, it denied **22.7 percent** of all such requests, an increase of **172 percent**." (footnote 71)

Report also (key-findings list):
> "UnitedHealthcare's prior authorization denial rate for post-acute care surged from **10.9 percent in 2020, to 16.3 percent in 2021, to 22.7 percent in 2022.** During this time, it was implementing multiple initiatives to automate the process."
- Note: 8.7% = 2019 baseline; 10.9% = 2020. Both true; 8.7%→22.7% is the full 2019–2022 span.

### VERBATIM — skilled nursing facility (the "ninefold" figure)
> "In 2019, the insurer issued an initial denial to **1.4 percent** of requests for admission to a skilled nursing facility. But in 2022 — the first full year in which naviHealth was managing them for UnitedHealthcare — the insurer denied **12.6 percent** of such requests: in other words, its 2022 denial rate for skilled nursing facilities was **nine times higher** than it was three years before."
- 2019: 1.4% (3,016 requests). 2022: 12.6% (34,359 requests).

### How the report ties denials to automation — CORRELATION, not proven causation (important for honest framing)
- Section heading (verbatim): **"UnitedHealthcare's denial rate for prior authorization requests for post-acute care significantly increased AT THE SAME TIME the company was launching initiatives to automate the process."**
- The report frames the relationship as **temporal / concurrent** ("during this time," "at the same time," "the first full year in which naviHealth was managing them") — it documents that denial rates rose as automation/naviHealth ramped, and cites internal documents, but does NOT claim a proven algorithmic causal mechanism.
- Internal-document specifics: "In April 2021, an internal UnitedHealthcare committee voted to approve the use of [automation initiative]..." and "In December of 2022, a UnitedHealthcare working group met to explore how to use AI and 'machine learning' to predict which denials of post-acute care cases were likely to be appealed." (Note: this Dec-2022 machine-learning effort was about predicting *appeals*, not the nH Predict length-of-stay tool.)
- On Humana: "Evidence obtained by the Subcommittee to date does not indicate the extent to which Humana may be using automation or predictive technologies to deny prior authorization requests." (Shows the subcommittee is careful about what it can/can't attribute to automation.)

### Comparative context (verbatim/near-verbatim from press release)
> "between 2019 and 2022, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and CVS each denied prior authorization requests for post-acute care at far higher rates than they did for other types of care."
- In 2022, UnitedHealthcare's and CVS's post-acute denial rates were ~3x their overall denial rates; Humana's was >16x its overall rate.
