# Raw capture — Lokken docket status + the discovery order (fetched 2026-07-07)

## Motion-to-dismiss outcome (Feb 13, 2025)
Per Healthcare Finance News / medicalmalpracticelawyers.com summaries of Doc. 91:
- Court **denied** UnitedHealth's motion to dismiss the **breach of contract** and **breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing** claims, finding they are NOT preempted by the federal Medicare Act (the court need only evaluate compliance with the insurance contracts themselves). Plaintiffs stated a viable theory that AI was used "in lieu of physicians."
- Other counts were dismissed. (ainvest earlier framed it as "dismissed 5 of 7 counts, allowed 2" — treat count-tally as REPORTED; the two surviving contract-based claims are confirmed by multiple sources.)

## The discovery order — ArentFox Schiff legal alert (primary-adjacent, detailed)
https://www.afslaw.com/perspectives/alerts/federal-court-orders-broad-discovery-against-uhc-ai-coverage-denial-lawsuit
- **Order date: March 9, 2026** (order on plaintiffs' motion to compel).
- **Scope: documents reaching back to January 2017 — roughly 9 years / "~a decade" of internal records.**
- UHC argued records **before July 2019** (the date nH Predict "first went into use") "lacked relevance." **The court rejected this**, holding earlier records are relevant as "circumstantial evidence of breach of contract."
- Categories UHC must produce:
  - Post-acute care claims policies/procedures; employee training materials
  - Documents **analyzing nH Predict**
  - Business records on the **naviHealth acquisition and projected cost savings**
  - Government-investigation materials
  - Performance reviews and compensation data for care coordinators and medical directors
  - **Internal AI review board materials** (including member identities)
  - Contact info for medical directors/care coordinators who denied coverage to ~300 class members
