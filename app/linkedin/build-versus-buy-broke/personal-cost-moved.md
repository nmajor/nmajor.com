Cancelling a software subscription doesn't delete the cost. It moves it onto you.

I build software for a living, and the savings stories going around right now all skip the same line.

The savings are real. A Retool survey this year found more than a third of teams have already replaced a paid tool with one they built themselves. AI did most of the work, and the license fees they cancelled were real money.

But not all the work happens before launch. The part that bites comes after.

Netlify's people team, four staff with no engineers among them, built their own HR tools and cut real spend. They were also honest about it. One app, a simple employee survey, pushed them past their depth on data security and forced them to pull in the company's engineering, security, and IT teams. Netlify is a software company. Its people had a security team down the hall.

If a survey tool tripped them, it can trip a logistics firm or a dental group with nobody to call.

Here is the part worth sitting with. When a vendor patched a hole at two in the morning, that was the rent. Cancel the license and you keep the savings, but that 2am job is now yours. Or it is no one's. And no one's is how an app nobody remembers ends up holding customer data behind a door nobody locked.

So build. Just own what you build, on purpose.

If your team has started building its own tools, who owns them after the first month?
