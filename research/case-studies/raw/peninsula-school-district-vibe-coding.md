Org: Peninsula School District, Gig Harbor, Washington. Public K-12 district
(~9,000 students, 17 schools per other public sources). A traditional, non-tech,
public-sector buyer - core to the beat.

What happened:
- District administrators, "most of whom do not have a background in computer science,"
  are using Claude Code ($200/month subscriptions each) to build their own software.
- Expects to save up to $250,000 in cancelled ed-tech contracts by the 2026-27 school year.
- Already identified "three or four" subscription tools it will not renew, including
  Informed K12 (a workflow automation tool for HR and finance).
- Also runs an internal "AI Studio" at ~$600/month using data-privacy agreements with
  Google Gemini, OpenAI ChatGPT, and Anthropic Claude.

Tools built:
- LessonLens - teachers upload lesson video/audio and get instructional feedback against
  coaching frameworks (giving precise directions, allowing thinking time). Soft launch ~April 2026.
- An interactive dashboard (in development) for the CTE department to streamline teacher
  purchasing requests.

People quoted:
- Kris Hagel, Chief Information Officer
- James Cantonwine, Director of Research and Assessment

Caveats / risks (named in the article):
- Hagel: "You can't just throw up any old thing that AI writes for you... There's got to
  be some vetting going on behind the scenes on that to make sure that it's being done safely."
- Article flags proving ROI and avoiding technical debt from unvetted AI-generated code.
- The $250k is an EXPECTED/projected saving for 2026-27, not yet realized. EdWeek frames
  it as ~$200k; K12 Dive as up to $250k - so treat as a ~$200-250k projection range.
