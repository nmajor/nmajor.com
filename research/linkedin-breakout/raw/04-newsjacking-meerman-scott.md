Book: David Meerman Scott, *Newsjacking: How to Inject your Ideas into a Breaking
News Story and Generate Tons of Media Coverage*, Wiley, 2011. Scott coined the term.

## Definition (verbatim)
"Newsjacking is the art and science of injecting your ideas into a breaking news
story so you and your ideas get noticed." "The process by which you inject your ideas
or angles into breaking news, in real-time, in order to generate media coverage for
yourself or your business."

## The core mechanics (verbatim / near-verbatim)
- **A level playing field that favors speed + observation:** "It creates a level
  playing field — literally anyone can newsjack — but that new level favors players
  who are observant, quick to react, and skilled at communicating." Big brand vs.
  small: "it doesn't matter if you are large and well known or tiny and obscure."
- **Timing is everything.** The value curve of a breaking story: media/audience
  attention spikes and then decays fast. Scott's model is that there is a window —
  after the news breaks but before the press has fully saturated the story — where an
  expert take can "draft off the news momentum." Get in during the second-derivative
  moment when journalists and buyers are actively searching for context/experts.
- **Why it works commercially:** "When there is news in your marketplace, reporters
  and analysts are looking for experts to comment on the story... As the story
  develops in real-time, buyers become interested in products and services based on
  what's happening now." So newsjacking generates both attention AND qualified
  demand: "Newsjacking drives new business... leads people directly into the buying
  process."
- **What a newsjack IS:** "You have a legitimate take on what's going on in the news
  and you create a piece of content on the web. That piece of content could be a
  video, a Tweet, a blog post, a LinkedIn update... Basically you say, 'I know
  something about this subject, and here's what I know about it.'"

## The honesty guardrail (Scott's own framing)
The word "legitimate take" is load-bearing. Scott repeatedly stresses AUTHENTIC
storytelling and a genuine, expert angle — not opportunistic hijacking of a tragedy.
The credible version is a real expert adding real context to a real, relevant story.
The failure mode (widely documented since 2011) is tone-deaf brand-jacking of
disasters, which destroys trust.
