## SOURCE: Search synthesis — honest/anti-hype B2B marketing builds trust
Sources: Sagefrog "B2B Technology Marketing Without the Hype"; Search Engine Journal "Addressing The B2B Trust Deficit: How To Win Buyers In 2026"; Intelemark ethics of appointment setting; Bluetext authentic B2B; The Insight Collective expert-led content.

Key (directly validates Nick's brand):
- "Hype might generate attention, but clarity generates trust, and trust converts. The companies that win won't be the loudest; they'll be the ones leading with honesty, specificity, and proof."
- Stats (FLAG: cited as Salesforce / McKinsey via marketing blogs — verify before quoting publicly): "80% of customers expect transparency from brands"; "transparent sales processes boost profits by 15%."
- B2B trust crisis: deep skepticism about ROI projections, business-case assumptions, sales authenticity. "41% of B2B tech buyers say they struggle to source content they can actually trust when bombarded by buzzwords, marketing hype, and conflicting claims." (FLAG: blog-cited stat.)
- STRONGEST anti-hype move (VERBATIM): "Saying 'we might not be right for you' is one of the strongest trust moves you can make, as it says you value their success over your sale."
- "Buyers reward specificity over spectacle, and thoughtful explainers over hype."
- Trust is built through consistency and proof, not volume or hype.

ON-BRAND PLAYBOOK IMPLICATIONS:
- The honest/anti-hype positioning is not a constraint — it's the wedge. The AI market is saturated with hype; credible, specific, proof-led content is the differentiator buyers are actively searching for.
- Use disqualification ("here's when you should NOT automate this", "you're not ready for AI yet — fix X first") as a trust signal. The AI readiness assessment naturally does this.
- Lead with specifics and proof (real workflows, real numbers, transparent error rates) — matches what 2026 AI buyers reward (see funnel-ai-consulting-niche-2026.md).

## TACTICS TO AVOID (sleazy / trust-burning) — compiled across all sources
- Pitch-first / service-listing DMs; bot-templated mass outreach; >4 message sequences.
- Aggressive hard CTAs on cold-audience content (destroys trust + tanks algo reach).
- Generic AI-generated lead magnets with no aha-moment / no actionability (feeds the trust deficit).
- Bold autonomy/ROI claims ("lights-out automation", "80% time savings") — the market now penalizes these; reality is 20-35% year-one time reduction.
- Fake scarcity, inflated ROI projections, unsourced stats presented as fact.
- Monetizing before any trust/value is established, or gating the offer on vanity follower counts.
